## Recommendations for Analyst
1. DUAL-PROP SAME-PLAYER MINUTES RISK RULE: When selecting two or more props on the same backup/secondary player in the same playoff game, require that the player's projected minutes (proj_min) exceed 25 AND that their L6 series minutes show no game with fewer than 20 minutes. If either condition fails, restrict to a maximum of one prop on that player (choose the highest-confidence tier). This rule directly addresses the Hartenstein REB+AST double miss, where both props were lost to the same unexpected 21-minute load — a shared single-point-of-failure that was not identified as a compound risk at selection time.
2. VOLATILE + IRON_FLOOR OVERRIDDEN + 72% FLOOR = CONDITIONAL SKIP FOR 3PM: When a 3PM prop carries all three of: (1) VOLATILE tag, (2) iron_floor explicitly overridden by VOLATILE, and (3) final confidence at or below 74%, treat this as a skip candidate rather than a minimum-floor emit. The Vassell 3PM miss demonstrates that the iron_floor override removes the structural safety net that justifies picking at minimum confidence levels — without iron_floor, a 72-74% 3PM pick in a road playoff game against elite defense has insufficient structural support. Require either iron_floor to be intact OR confidence above 78% before emitting a VOLATILE-tagged 3PM prop.
3. BACKUP CENTER G7 MINUTES VOLATILITY ADJUSTMENT: For backup centers (raw_avgs PTS < 15 and role = reserve/backup) in a G7 or elimination game, apply an additional -5pp confidence penalty to ALL counting stat props (REB, AST, PTS) unless the player has logged 28+ minutes in at least 4 of the last 6 series games. The rationale: G7 game scripts often produce unexpected role compression for backup bigs when coaches shorten rotations or when game flow demands defensive matchup changes. Hartenstein's 21-minute G7 load occurred in a CLOSE game without a blowout explanation — pure rotation compression. This penalty applies on top of existing VOLATILE and trend penalties.
4. WCF/SERIES WITHIN-SERIES STREAK DECAY FOR BACKUP BIGS: When a backup player (proj_min < 28) has a within-series perfect streak (e.g., 6/6 at T2) but their most recent two series games show a miss or minimum hit at the threshold (e.g., Hartenstein G5=2 REB, G6=2 AST exactly at threshold), apply a within-series recency decay: discount the 6/6 streak to an effective 4/6 for confidence calculation purposes. Back-to-back minimum hits signal the player is approaching their floor, not comfortably clearing it. This would have reduced Hartenstein's AST confidence below 72% and potentially triggered a skip.
