## Lessons
- Low-minute rotation players (proj_min ≤ 22, min_floor < 16) must not be included in PTS picks even under elimination-game override logic: Coby White's 17 actual minutes in the blowout confirm that the elimination override does not protect players whose role is inherently volume-contingent — the 4 actual PTS on 1-5 FG was a product of both cold shooting AND a game script that compressed his opportunity to zero in the second half. The override is designed for starters and primary rotation players with proj_min ≥ 30, not for fringe bench contributors.
- Non-iron-floor 3PM props on secondary/fringe rotation players in blowout-risk games should not be selected, regardless of 10/10 historical hit rate: Coby White's 3PM T1 miss (0 threes in 17 min) is directly explained by the same minute compression that killed his PTS pick — a 10/10 hit rate on a 3PM T1 is only realizable if the player gets meaningful run, and a proj_min of 22 in an elimination road game for the underdog carries structural blowout-exit risk that voids the sample base. The 3PM prop should have been skipped when the PTS prop already flagged minutes risk.
- 3PM props on players in blowout games are the highest-variance prop type on the slate: the session produced 1/3 on 3PM (33.3%), with both misses (White 3PM, Bane 3PM) occurring in games where the player either lost minutes (White, blowout loser) or simply went cold across a full 35-minute sample (Bane). 3PM props require both opportunity and execution; a 9/10 historical rate (Bane) does not immunize against cold-shooting nights, and the non-iron-floor 80% cap correctly bounded confidence — but the prop type itself is systematically volatile in high-stakes game contexts and should require iron_floor or a 9+/10 at the exact tier (not overall) before selection.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Institute a hard proj_min ≥ 28 floor for any PTS or 3PM pick on non-iron-floor players, even under elimination-game override: Coby White (proj_min=22, min_floor=15.8) should have been skipped on both props — the elimination override only suspends the step-down rule, it does not eliminate the underlying minutes-volume risk for players with thin average minutes. Add a rule: if proj_min < 28 AND iron_floor = false AND prop_type IN (PTS, 3PM) → mandatory skip regardless of override context.
2. When two picks on the same player (White PTS + White 3PM) share the same structural minutes fragility warning, skip both picks and do not use either as a parlay anchor: the cannibalization of the same risk factor across two props compounds the miss probability and adds no diversification. Add a rule: if any pick triggers a proj_min fragility flag → suppress all other props for the same player on that slate.
3. Apply a blowout_spread_gate to non-iron-floor secondary/tertiary scorer props when the pre-game spread exceeds 5.5 points AND the player's proj_min is below 28: CHA was a +3.5 underdog (spread below the formal blowout_risk gate of 8), so no flag fired — but the actual margin was 31 points. Consider lowering the blowout_risk activation threshold from spread_abs ≥ 8 to spread_abs ≥ 5 for secondary scorers with min_floor < 20, or require a minimum blowout_games resilience rate of ≥ 70% before selecting these players on the road as underdogs.
4. Non-iron-floor 3PM props with a consistent tag but no pace-context confirmation should be capped at 75% confidence (not 80%) when the player's prop is in a game where the team is an underdog and the opponent is at home in an elimination game: Bane's 3PM pick was capped at 80% (non-iron-floor ceiling), but the home elimination game for ORL — where pace compression and defensive intensity typically suppress three-point attempt volume for visiting-team shooters — warranted an extra -5% deduction. Add a rule: non-iron-floor 3PM in away elimination game OR blowout-risk spread context → cap at 75%.
5. Classify the injury-exit miss (Brandon Miller AST) cleanly in the workflow and do not attempt to retroactively apply a model rule: the post-game event confirms Miller exited at 29 minutes with a head injury and underwent season-ending surgery. The 1 actual assist vs T2 is entirely explained by the injury exit, not by the soft_floor or tough-defense adjustments. No rule change is warranted; confirm the injury_event classification is logged so the Miller AST pick does not pollute the vs-ORL tough-defense signal analysis.
