1. Lower the blowout_secondary_scorer hard-skip threshold from spread_abs ≥15 to spread_abs ≥13.5 for non-iron-floor secondary scorers on PTS props. The Bane miss (actual=2, spread=14.5) demonstrates that a 0.5-point buffer below a rigid threshold creates exactly the blowout minute-compression risk the rule was designed to prevent. At 74% confidence (blowout cap), the pick had marginal edge — a tighter hard-skip threshold eliminates the worst-case outcomes for secondary scorers in near-blowout spreads.
2. Begin tracking DET as a potential AST suppressor candidate. Both Knueppel AST T2 (1 assist) and Brandon Miller AST T2 (0 assists) missed on the same slate against DET (n=2, 0% AST hit rate this game). While this is a single-game sample, DET's defensive profile under Bickerstaff appears to compress secondary-creator assist sequences in the same way MIN's switching scheme does. Flag DET×AST for a watch item and apply extra scrutiny (prefer T2 iron-floor only, or require 9+/10 hit rate) until at least 8 AST picks vs DET are graded.
3. For AST props on winning-side blowout teams: when the pre-game spread is 5-10 points (competitive pre-game) but the team wins by 20+, non-primary creators' assist totals are compressed by creation-sequence collapse even with full minutes. NAW (1 assist in 32 min, ATL +22 blowout win) is the prototype case. The system should apply a post-hoc caution: if a player is a non-primary creator (best_tier AST ≤ T2, raw_avgs AST < 4.0) and is on a team that was not a heavy pre-game favorite, their AST pick is more vulnerable to winning-side blowout script than the pre-game spread suggests. Consider a winning-blowout-script secondary-creator penalty of -5% when spread_abs < 10 but game develops into a 20+ margin.
4. Validate the afternoon lineup amendment agent's upside-revision accuracy: on this slate, all three revised-up picks (Barnes PTS/REB after Barrett OUT, Sengun PTS and Thompson REB after Randle/Reid OUT) hit decisively. The agent correctly identified frontcourt scoring and rebounding upside when key opposing bigs were absent. Reinforce this pattern — future slates should explicitly prioritize interior scoring (PTS T10+) and REB picks vs. teams missing multiple frontcourt players, as the signal is confirmed reliable across this sample.
5. For iron-floor AST T2 picks with trend=down on non-primary creators against tough defenses: consider adding a rule that when (a) player is non-PG or non-primary ball-handler, (b) trend=down on AST, and (c) opp_defense=tough, the iron-floor tag should not fully suppress the trend=down penalty — apply -5% to the base before the iron-floor cap is set. Brandon Miller (iron_floor, consistent, 95% overall, trend=down, vs_tough=82%) produced 0 assists; the iron-floor blocked all downside signals. A modified rule: iron-floor protects the tier selection but allows a -5% confidence deduction for trend=down on non-primary creators vs tough defenses.
