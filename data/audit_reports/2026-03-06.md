## Recommendations for Analyst
1. When a player's trend for a specific prop type is marked 'down', do not select that prop type even if the overall hit rate is high (≥80%). Durant's 3PM miss is a direct example: 9/10 overall hit rate meant nothing on a night the trend was declining. Institute a rule: 'down' trend on 3PM = skip or step down one full tier regardless of aggregate rate.
2. Reassess MIA as 'mid' for REB props — MIA's defensive profile explicitly suppresses opponent rebounds via zone scheme and elite defensive glass. Any wing or forward REB prop against MIA should be rated 'tough' by default. Apply this correction to Knueppel-type picks immediately: wings with T4 REB against MIA should be skipped or stepped down to T2–T3.
3. For AST props on big-man hub players (Sengun, Jokic types), add a game-script risk flag: if the team is heavily favored and a blowout is likely, hub-big AST volume compresses in the second half. Against depleted opponents like POR, the blowout risk that protects PTS floors (more usage early) actually suppresses AST because the game is sealed before the passing game fully develops. Consider capping big-man AST confidence at 72% max when blowout risk is present.
4. For guard PTS props against NYK (low pace, elite rebounding suppression), apply a mandatory tier step-down of one level from the analytically selected floor. Murray at T20 was a reasonable pick in most matchups but NYK's pace and scheme profile consistently suppresses guard counting stats. Any guard PTS pick vs NYK should be evaluated at the next tier down before finalizing.
5. The two NO_DATA results (LeBron James PTS and AST vs IND) represent ungradeable picks that consumed confidence budget. Before finalizing any pick on a player with recent availability ambiguity, require a confirmed active status signal within 2 hours of tip. If confirmation is unavailable, exclude the player from the card rather than submitting picks that may result in NO_DATA outcomes — especially for parlays where a DNP would void or lose the leg.
