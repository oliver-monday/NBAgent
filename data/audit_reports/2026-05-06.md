## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a compounding blowout penalty for AST picks on the WINNING side of games where spread_abs ≥ 8.0: if spread_abs ≥ 8.0 in playoffs, apply an additional -7pp beyond the standard blowout penalty for AST T4+ on the favored team's players. This addresses the Fox AST miss directly — the current -5pp blowout penalty left the pick at 80% confidence despite the structural risk of starters being managed once a large lead is secured. The Fox case (9/10 overall, H2H MIN 3/3, but 26 min in a 38-point win with 2 AST) confirms the existing penalty is too soft for large-spread playoff games.
2. For 3PM picks where the tier is T1 and no iron_floor is present: require evidence of 3PM ATTEMPT VOLUME in recent games, not just hit rate. If a player's recent L5 shows fewer than 3 three-point attempts per game on average, apply an additional -5pp regardless of hit rate, because T1 3PM can fail not from shooting percentage but from attempt scarcity in blowout or role-compressed games. The Castle 3PM miss is the clearest example: 0-for-1 with no shooting misses at the tier level in a game where attempt volume collapsed due to blowout script.
3. For picks on QUESTIONABLE players who are projected to be on the LOSING SIDE of the game (opponent spread favorite by 7+), compound the injury penalty with a blowout-minutes penalty: apply an additional -5pp to PTS and AST props on top of the standard QUESTIONABLE -5pp. A QUESTIONABLE player on the underdog side in a potential blowout faces two independent minutes compression mechanisms — injury-related load management AND losing-side game-script truncation. The Edwards PTS and AST misses both suffered from this compounding effect that was not operationalized at pick time.
4. The MIN×AST suppressor's interaction with blowout game-script should be formalized: when SAS (or any team with a floor compression pattern) is the favored home team by 8+ points in a playoff game, AST picks on the LOSING TEAM'S players should receive the MIN×AST suppressor penalty stacked on top of the blowout-AST penalty. In the SAS@MIN G2 context, Fox's 2 AST is consistent with both the blowout losing-side mechanism AND the suppressor being neutralized when the game runs away — but picks made before the game cannot know which mechanism will dominate. The double-penalty approach accounts for this structural uncertainty.
5. The 76-80% confidence band underperformed significantly (66.7% actual vs 78.0% expected, 6/9). All four misses occurred in this band (Fox AST at 80%, Edwards PTS at 74%, Edwards AST at 78%, Castle 3PM at 80%). This is a sample-size-limited signal but warrants monitoring: consider whether the blowout game-script was systematically underpenalized in the 76-80% band specifically. For the next run, picks in this band that combine (a) blowout_risk=true AND (b) spread_abs ≥ 8.0 should be capped at 75% confidence or require iron_floor to proceed above 75%.
