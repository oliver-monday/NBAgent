## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Institute a hard skip on Jokic REB T12 when MIN×rebounding competition (specifically Gobert at full health) is present in the same game and the VOLATILE tag is active. The G5 16-AST pick demonstrated that Jokic's playmaking overrides the MIN×AST suppressor in competitive scripts — but his rebounding is structurally competed by Gobert in a way the quant H2H signal does not isolate. Add a rebounding_competition_flag check: if opponent center avg REB ≥ 10.0 in series and VOLATILE tag is present, apply an additional -5pp penalty or require 10/10 H2H rate at the target tier (not 9/10) before selecting Jokic REB T12+.
2. Apply the CLV ast_lost_close warning signal (-0.5pp threshold) as a mandatory confidence penalty of -3pp on AST props where the warning fires, rather than the current 'applied: false' status. Four AST picks this slate (Murray AST T4, Maxey AST T6, Brunson AST T4, White AST T2) all had active CLV warnings with penalties not applied. Three of those four hit, but White AST T2 missed — and the warning was the only live pre-game signal that the market had already downgraded the probability. Applying a -3pp mechanical penalty would reduce confidence without flipping any pick to skip, while creating an accurate record of market disagreement.
3. For Murray PTS props: when both FG_COLD (≥15%) AND FG_MARGIN_THIN/NEG fire simultaneously on a VOLATILE player, treat the double-step cascade (T25→T20→T15) as the maximum selection floor, and require series hit rate at the walked tier to be ≥80% (not just 70%) before selecting. Murray's series 5/5 at T15 was the justifying signal, but his actual output (12 pts in 40 min) shows that even T15 was too aggressive when both shooting suppression flags are simultaneously active. A combined-flag threshold of 80% series hit rate would have kept Murray at T15 as a borderline pass but surfaced the risk more explicitly.
4. Brown REB T4 is a case where the PHI system-wide suppressor's mechanism — scheme-based disruption of offensive flow — plausibly compresses non-primary-scorer rebounding through fewer BOS offensive possessions and disrupted rhythm. Add a cross-prop PHI suppressor note: for BOS secondary contributors (non-Tatum, non-Brown primary) at REB T4 in PHI suppressor context, apply an additional -3pp penalty to reflect that PHI's scheme reduces BOS possessions generally, not only scoring actions. This would not have changed the Brown REB pick direction (T4 stayed selected) but would have appropriately reduced confidence from 76% to ~73%, keeping it in the lower band.
5. For any pick where the CLV ast_lost_close warning fires AND the morning_implied_prob is ≥90% (a very high market consensus price), treat this combination as a mandatory 'FADE' override regardless of the Analyst's calibrated_edge calculation — even if calibrated_edge is positive. Murray AST T4 (morning 94.7%, market 94.1%, CLV -0.62pp) and White AST T2 (morning 96.2%, market 93.3%, CLV -2.82pp) both had morning implied probs above 93% with CLV moving against the pick. When the market prices a prop above 93% and then moves further against it, the pick has near-zero positive EV even if the hit-rate quant supports it. Automate a FADE recommendation (not skip from the slate, but flag for the bet_recommendation engine) when morning_implied_prob ≥ 93% AND clv_warning.live_clv_pp < -0.5.
