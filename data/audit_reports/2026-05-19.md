## Lessons
- Harden AST T4 in a G1 series-opener context: the H27 'bulletproof' AST designation (100% in mid/late playoff games) was misapplied to a G1 opening game where no within-series baseline exists. The dossier update explicitly states that Harden's AST is stable WITHIN a series but consistently suppressed vs regular-season averages (~1 below RS career), and that high tiers (T6+) are not supported by career playoff data. In G1 of a new series, the within-series stability anchor has no foundation — the pick at 80% at T4 effectively treated G1 as a mid-series game, which was analytically incorrect. The 8/10 hit_rate_display reflects the full-season quant window, but the series-specific (R2 vs NYK) baseline was zero at pick time, and the general playoff suppression signal should have driven confidence closer to 72-75% rather than 80%.
- The post-game news notes a possible lingering hand injury from the prior series that may have affected Harden's playmaking in G1. This was not flagged in the injury report (NOT_LISTED at check time) and therefore could not have been incorporated into the pick. However, when carry-over injury language appears in post-series news, the workflow should flag this as a risk factor for the opening game of the subsequent series — particularly for a playmaking-dependent prop like AST.
