## Recommendations for Analyst
1. CHRONIC INJURY + BLOWOUT COMPOUND SKIP: When a player carries a documented multi-game chronic injury (e.g. Fox's ankle confirmed since prior series) AND the pre-game spread implies meaningful blowout risk (spread_abs ≥ 8 in a game where the injured player's team is expected to win large), apply a hard skip on PTS props regardless of tier and H2H hit rate. The chronic ankle injury suppresses both minutes and shooting efficiency in garbage time in a way that H2H data from earlier in the series cannot capture — Fox's 1-for-9 from the field in 26 blowout minutes is the prototype failure for this compound scenario.
2. QUES + PRIOR SEVERE MINUTES RESTRICTION = SKIP THRESHOLD FOR COUNTING STATS: When a player is listed QUESTIONABLE with a specific soft-tissue injury (hamstring, ankle, knee) AND has a documented game in the current series where they played fewer than 15 minutes due to that same injury (Williams: 7 min in G3, 10 min in G6), both their PTS and AST props should be classified as skip-eligible at any confidence level below 80%. The binary tail risk of a repeat restriction is not adequately penalized by a -5pp QUES deduction alone — the prior severe restriction game should trigger a hard skip gate rather than a confidence haircut.
3. BLOWOUT GAME-SCRIPT MINIMUM-TIER REB SKIP: For REB picks at the minimum valid tier (T4 or T6 minimum) with VOLATILE tag AND a pre-game spread_abs ≥ 15 (clear blowout expectation), apply a hard skip rather than emitting at the 72% floor confidence. Vassell REB T4 and Hartenstein REB T6 both missed primarily because blowout garbage-time compressed their minutes to 26 and 16 respectively — the minimum-tier confidence floor was not designed to absorb the full downside of blowout minute compression for secondary players. The 25th-pct gate provides some protection but does not gate against a 10–16 minute blowout appearance.
4. NON-IRON_FLOOR 3PM IN BLOWOUT CONTEXT: Castle 3PM T1 (trend=down, non-iron_floor) should have been evaluated against the pre-game spread; with SAS as a heavy home favorite in an elimination game where the blowout script was a realistic outcome, non-iron_floor 3PM picks with trend=down should require either iron_floor designation OR a competitive game script expectation (spread_abs ≤ 8) before emitting. The blowout reduced meaningful possessions for Castle in the fourth quarter and garbage-time lineups reduced 3PM opportunities.
5. AMENDMENT UPSIDE SIGNAL ON QUES PLAYERS: For the two Williams picks, the lineup_update flagged direction='unchanged' at pick time (the amendment was triggered by Williams being QUES but revised_confidence stayed at 70%). When the lineup_update.triggered_by includes the same player as the pick subject and that player is QUES, the amendment agent should force a re-evaluation of the skip threshold rather than confirming 'unchanged' — the amendment correctly identified the risk but did not translate it into a skip recommendation; this feedback loop should be tightened so QUES self-referential triggers automatically escalate to skip review.
