## Recommendations for Analyst
1. COMPOUND SKIP RULE — trend=down + active injury context on 3PM: When a player carries trend=down on 3PM AND there is any documented mid-series injury (even NOT_LISTED at pick time) affecting a shooting-relevant body part (ankle, wrist, hand), treat the combination as a hard skip at T1 regardless of recent hit rate. Do not apply the 9+/10 exception when both signals are present simultaneously. Fox 3PM T1 demonstrates that a 10/11 recent rate can be built on pre-injury games, making the exception threshold stale. Operationalize: check for any WEB_RECAP or series diary injury reference before invoking the trend=down T1 exception.
2. SCORING-MODE AST SUPPRESSION RULE — primary playmaker in shot-heavy game scripts: For guards with raw_avgs AST ≥ 6.0 who are also primary scorers (raw_avgs PTS ≥ 25), add a formal 'scoring-mode override' signal: when a player's recent playoff games show PTS/AST inverse correlation (high scoring = low AST pattern visible in series diary), apply an additional -5pp penalty to any AST T6+ pick regardless of vs_tough or H2H data. Brunson's series history shows this pattern explicitly — his 30-pt G1 produced only 2 AST. The competitive=67% at T6 already flagged the risk; a formal scoring-mode suppression rule would have pushed confidence below the AST floor and triggered a skip.
3. WALK-DOWN EXECUTION DISCIPLINE — reasoning_override contradictions must void the pick: When the analyst's reasoning_override field explicitly states 'this pick should be at a different tier/value than walked_tier,' the pick must be suppressed and reissued at the corrected tier before running. Brunson AST ran at T6/pick_value=6 despite the analyst documenting mid-reasoning that T4 was the correct selection. Implement a validation check: if reasoning_override is present and contains corrected tier language, flag the pick as NEEDS_REISSUE and do not run it until corrected. This is a workflow integrity issue, not an analytical gap.
4. vs_TOUGH DEGRADATION THRESHOLD — formalize the mandatory step-down trigger: When vs_tough drops more than 15pp below overall hit rate at the selected tier, treat this as a mandatory step-down signal equivalent to FG_MARGIN_THIN — not a discretionary penalty. Vassell PTS T10 showed vs_tough=73% vs overall=90% (17pp gap) and the penalty applied was only -7pp, leaving confidence at 78% and the pick running at T10. A mandatory step-down to T7 (or skip if no market exists) when the vs_tough gap exceeds 15pp would have prevented this near-miss. This rule applies to all prop types where opp_defense is a rated input (PTS, AST) — not to REB or 3PM where opp_defense is treated as noise.
5. FINALS SERIES OPENER — new-opponent adjustment for trend=down props: In the first game of any new series (G1), apply an additional -3pp to any pick with trend=down regardless of prop type. The trend=down tag reflects performance trajectory against recent opponents, but a new opponent in a new series (especially one as defensively distinct as SAS vs NYK, or NYK vs SAS) introduces structural uncertainty not captured in cross-series hit rates. This would have further suppressed Fox 3PM (already borderline at 78%), OG 3PM (already at 75%), and Castle REB (already at 72%), helping calibrate G1 slate confidence more conservatively while preserving iron_floor and 9+/10 exceptions for truly structural props.
