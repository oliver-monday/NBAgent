## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Codify a blowout-risk AST compression rule for primary playmakers on the LOSING side: when a pick's team enters a game as the underdog and the pre-game spread exceeds 4 points, apply an additional -5pp AST confidence penalty for T6+ picks even for primary creators (raw_avgs AST ≥ 8.0). The Cunningham AST T6 miss demonstrates that a 9/10 H2H hit rate and 6/6 series hit rate cannot override the structural AST suppression that occurs when the losing team's game script is disrupted by a 20+ point deficit. The existing blowout penalty applies to PTS but not explicitly to AST at the high-tier level for playmakers — this gap should be closed.
2. Introduce a pre-existing injury carry-forward scan for late-series games: when a player has a documented injury from a prior game in the same series (e.g. Harris left ankle sprain confirmed in Game 5), the system should flag this as an injury risk modifier even if the player is NOT_LISTED on the game-day injury report. Specifically, if series diary notes or post-game news from a prior game in the same series reference a structural injury (sprain, bruise, strain), reduce PTS and 3PM confidence by 5-10pp and flag the pick as requiring manual injury confirmation before run. This would have reduced both Harris picks below their 75%/72% confidence levels and potentially triggered a skip.
3. Strengthen the losing-side blowout interaction with FG_MARGIN_THIN for PTS picks: Cunningham's PTS T15 miss occurred because FG_MARGIN_THIN:10% was at the exact boundary and the system treated it as not triggering a mandatory step-down. Tighten the FG_MARGIN_THIN threshold to strictly < 15% (not < 10%) for players who are projected to be on the losing side of a game with pre-game spread ≥ 4 points. This creates an earlier safety valve for players with thin FG cushion in unfavorable game scripts, without broadly tightening the rule for all contexts.
4. Do not treat 3PM T1 picks on players carrying known injury risk as structurally safe: the Harris 3PM T1 miss reinforces that T1 3PM picks (pick_value=1, a very low bar) carry meaningful tail risk when the player has a lower-extremity injury limiting their lateral movement and shot mechanics. When a player has a documented ankle or knee issue in the same series, even T1 3PM picks should be treated as SKIP-eligible unless there is explicit game-day confirmation of full functionality. The SHORT_SAMPLE:6g flag already correctly reduced Harris 3PM confidence — the additional layer should be the injury carry-forward check described above.
5. For future G7 elimination games involving a heavy underdog (spread ≥ 4 points against), treat losing-side PTS and AST picks above T10/T4 respectively as requiring iron_floor or 9/10 hit rate confirmation rather than 7/10 or 8/10. The Cunningham slate (PTS T15, AST T6) illustrates that even a 6/6 series hit rate can be neutralized by a blowout game script when the team is outmatched — the confidence gate for heavy-underdog elimination game picks should be stricter than for competitive or home-favorite elimination games.
