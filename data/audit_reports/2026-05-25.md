The Analyst applied a -5pp late-series AST dossier penalty but did not apply a blowout risk discount. The actual outcome — zero threes on six attempts in a blowout loss — is the worst-case realization of the structural suppression signal. The Analyst correctly identified all individual risk factors but assembled them into a floor pick rather than a skip.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. For the Mitchell AST miss: introduce a blowout-risk compounding rule for AST picks on players whose teams are heavy elimination underdogs in a close-out game, even when pre-game spread is narrow. Specifically: when a player is on the losing team in an elimination context AND the game ultimately becomes a BLOWOUT (margin ≥20), AST production is structurally suppressed in Q4 garbage time. Since spread alone cannot predict blowout outcomes, apply an additional -5pp confidence penalty to AST T2+ picks in close-out games where the player's team is the trailing-series side (i.e., facing series elimination), to account for the elevated blowout tail risk in desperation games that go wrong.
2. For the Harden 3PM miss: when three or more compounding 3PM suppression signals fire simultaneously — (1) career playoff 3PM trajectory monotonic_down, (2) trend=down quant tag, and (3) player team is facing elimination or is the losing-series side — treat this combination as a mandatory SKIP rather than a floor pick at 75%. The 9/10 overall exception that holds a step-down at T1 should be overrideable by a compound suppression trigger: if career playoff 3PM CAUTION + trend=down fire together, the pick must be skipped regardless of overall T1 hit rate. This prevents the 75% floor from functioning as a soft landing pad for structurally fragile 3PM props.
3. Reinforce the existing LATE_FADER game-in-series threshold discipline: the Analyst correctly did not fire the LATE_FADER modifier on either Anunoby PTS or Mobley PTS in Game 4 (modifier activates at Game 5). This precision should be maintained — do not pre-apply LATE_FADER before Game 5 even when a series is 3-0 and Game 4 is a potential sweep game. The sweep context is already captured via the informational conservative haircut process; double-penalizing before the threshold is overfitting.
4. For AST props on players whose teams are in a blowout-elimination risk scenario (close-out game, trailing in series), add an explicit blowout_losing_side_AST_cap: reduce maximum confidence to 80% for any AST pick, regardless of vs_tough hit rate or overall hit rate. This is narrower than the full blowout_secondary_scorer rule and targets the specific mechanism: the losing team's star being benched in Q4 with the game out of reach, eliminating late-game facilitation opportunities that dominate AST counts.
5. Validate the no_data pipeline coverage for blowout games: seven of eleven picks today returned NO_DATA box score entries despite the game being completed. Inferred post-game news was the primary classification mechanism. The audit pipeline should flag when a game's margin ≥20 and more than 50% of picks in that game return NO_DATA, as blowout garbage-time substitutions and unusual stat lines are precisely the scenarios most likely to generate pipeline gaps. Ensuring confirmed box score data rather than inferred entries for blowout games would improve miss classification accuracy.
