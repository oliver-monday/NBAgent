## Lessons
- Jalen Johnson's AST miss (actual 3 vs T6) in a 22-point blowout loss for ATL is a clear model_gap_rule event: the BLOWOUT_RISK signal was present (ATL was a road underdog, HOU won by 22), yet no AST-specific blowout penalty was applied to Johnson's pick despite his role as the primary playmaker — losing-side blowout scripts compress half-court possession count and structured creation opportunities for ball-handlers, and this must trigger an AST confidence penalty equivalent to the PTS blowout penalty when the player is on the losing side of a spread ≥10.
- Johnson's PTS miss (actual 14 vs T15) in the same blowout context is borderline variance/model_gap_rule: a 9/10 player at T15 missing by 1 point in a 22-point blowout loss is not a pure selection error, but the blowout-loss context (ATL trailing by large margins in the second half) structurally reduces the primary forward's scoring volume — the model's blowout penalty for PTS should account for losing-side players, not just winning-side star-rest scenarios.
- AST props at T6 and above carry meaningful single-game variance (Johnson's sigma=0.366 was flagged in trim_reasons) — the 74% confidence on an 8/10 AST T6 was reasonable in isolation, but the combination of volatile AST, road underdog status, and blowout risk created a correlated downside that the individual pick confidence did not fully price. When two or more of these factors co-occur on an AST prop at T6+, apply an additional 5pp discount or require 9+/10 hit rate to proceed.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a losing-side blowout AST penalty: when a player's team is the underdog and the pre-game spread implies a potential blowout (spread_abs ≥ 10 against them), apply a -10pp AST confidence penalty equivalent to the existing winning-side blowout PTS penalty. Johnson's 74% AST T6 pick should have been flagged to ~64% or skipped entirely given ATL's road underdog spread context.
2. For AST props at T6 and above on non-elite playmakers (raw_avgs AST < 8.0), require at least 9/10 recent hit rate AND soft or mid defense to proceed at any confidence above 70%. Johnson's 8/10 / T6 / mid-defense / volatile combination is a prototype case where the tier ceiling rule should impose a higher gate than currently enforced.
3. Validate the HOU blowout-win star-minutes pattern going forward: Sengun and Thompson both played 32–34 minutes in a 22-point HOU win, suggesting HOU does not heavily rest starters in blowout wins. If this pattern holds over ≥5 HOU blowout wins, create a HOU-specific blowout exception rule that does not apply the winning-side star-rest penalty to HOU rotation players.
4. The Jokic VOLATILE+AST T8 hard skip was correct procedurally (pick still hit 9/0), but the audit notes the hit — track VOLATILE+AST hard-skipped picks separately to evaluate whether the skip rule is correctly calibrated for elite playmakers with iron-floor PTS tags. If VOLATILE+AST skips hit at ≥70% over 10+ skip events, consider a tiered exemption for players with raw_avgs AST ≥ 8.0 and iron_floor=true on PTS.
5. Brown's T15 PTS pick at 74% with spread_abs=15.5 hit (actual 30) — but White's identical spread context (T10, 74%, same game) also hit (actual 14). Before applying the manual_skip override to secondary scorers in large-spread games, confirm whether the game-script risk materialized: BOS won by only 5, meaning the blowout-compression scenario never occurred. Flag large-spread picks for post-game review and track actual margin vs. predicted margin — if spread_abs ≥ 15 games regularly close to <10 margin, the blowout risk discount may be structurally over-applied.
