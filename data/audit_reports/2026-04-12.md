## Lessons
- PTS props in regular-season finales carry structural minutes-compression risk that the system does not fully price. Four of the five misses today (Quickley, Bridges, Cunningham, Bane PTS) involved players whose minutes were actively managed down — Quickley to 17 min, Bridges to 24 min, Cunningham to 22 min, Bane to 18 min — all in games where the outcome was settled or where playoff health was the priority. The system applied blowout caps but the MINUTES MANAGEMENT dimension of end-of-season games is distinct from in-season blowout garbage time and is not currently modeled as a separate suppressor.
- Secondary scorer PTS T10 passes the floor exception but does not escape the underlying risk: Quickley (4 actual vs T10), Bane PTS (8 actual vs T10), and Bridges (7 actual vs T10) all missed as secondary scorers in games with large spreads or minute-management contexts. The T10 floor exception prevents the hard skip from firing but does not raise the underlying probability — when multiple structural risks compound (secondary scorer + season finale + blowout spread), the system's 74% cap may still be too permissive.
- The VOLATILE tag combined with post-season load management produced the Cunningham miss: played only 22 minutes in an 81-63 halftime blowout. The VOLATILE deduction and blowout-resilient offset landed at 72%, which was sound analytically, but the season-finale playoff-rest dynamic (DET leading by 18+ at half, explicitly managing Cade for playoff health) was a structural miss factor. When a team has clinched seeding and faces a weaker opponent with a large pre-game spread on the final day of the regular season, an additional minutes-management risk flag should fire for star starters.
- Desmond Bane's PTS T10 miss repeated the exact pattern documented in prior audit history — secondary scorer in an ORL blowout-risk game, pulled early with limited minutes (18 minutes in a game ORL lost 113-108, which was actually closer than expected). The human verdict was manual_skip, confirming the Analyst already identified this risk. This is the second consecutive documented Bane blowout PTS miss. The system should now hard-skip Bane PTS at any spread ≥ 10 when ORL is favored, not just at the T25+ volatile_weak_combo threshold — the Bane secondary scorer blowout pattern is confirmed across multiple instances.
