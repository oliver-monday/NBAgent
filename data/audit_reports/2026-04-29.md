## Lessons
- Alperen Sengun PTS T15 miss (actual 14, miss by 1): The system selected T15 despite FG_MARGIN_THIN at 8% (below the 10% floor) triggering a step-down that could not walk to T10 because no T10 market existed. When the mandatory step-down tier has no available market and the analyst is forced to stay at T15, the confidence penalty applied (–2%) was insufficient to reflect the true risk of a thin-margin scorer missing by exactly 1 point at his baseline tier. The LAL system-wide suppressor was active for HOU players — though Sengun's opp_today was rated 'mid,' the LAL suppressor context applies even to interior scorers and adds tail risk. The 72% confidence pick was borderline at best given the combined constraints.
- LeBron James REB T4 miss (actual 3, miss by 1): The offensive-first player gate correctly stepped this from T6 to T4, and the VOLATILE tag was applied. However, the series 4/4 data at T4 created false confidence in an 'unbreakable floor.' LeBron grabbed only 3 rebounds in 39 minutes in a CLOSE game (HOU won by 6) — the miss was not game-script driven. The HOU system-wide suppressor was active, and HOU's league-best rebounding percentage (54.5% OREB) directly reduces opponent second-chance opportunities and weakens counting rebound floors for LAL players. This opponent-REB-compression mechanism is not captured in the current quant pipeline for REB props.
- Evan Mobley AST T2 miss (actual 1, miss by 1): The dossier CAUTION directive correctly blocked T4+ and selected T2. However, Mobley's career playoff AST CAUTION (3/3 prior playoffs below RS career, trajectory flat per dossier) combined with the CLE game script (CLE won by 5, competitive game where Mobley had 33 minutes) still produced only 1 AST. The H2H 7/7 at T2 and 90% overall masked the structural cross-season suppression at T2 that the dossier's formal caution only begins at T4. The miss at T2 suggests the AST suppression is deeper than the avoid-T4+ directive alone captures — the dossier should be read as 'prefer T2 only with strong confirmation,' not as a reliable floor.
- Brandon Ingram PTS T10 miss (actual 1, injury exit): Post-game news confirmed Ingram exited Game 5 with right heel inflammation after only 11 minutes. The pick object has post_game_event = 'injury_exit' annotated directly. This is an injury_event classification — not an analytical failure. However, the pick's context warrants noting: Ingram was flagged with FG_COLD (–17%), VOLATILE, and a minimum-confidence 70% pick. The post_game_event annotation confirms the heel inflammation was the causal factor, not pick selection logic.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. When FG_MARGIN_THIN triggers a mandatory step-down but the lower-tier market does not exist (as with Sengun T15 where no T10 market was available), treat the inability to step down as a near-skip signal — apply an additional –5% confidence penalty beyond the current –2%, and require the residual confidence to clear 75% to proceed. If it falls below 75% after this enhanced penalty, skip the pick. This rule would have reduced or eliminated the Sengun T15 marginal pick.
2. Add an opponent-REB-compression modifier for teams ranked top-5 in OREB% (like HOU at league-best 54.5%). When picking opponent player REB props against such teams, apply an additional –5% confidence deduction and require the 3rd-lowest floor gate to use a stricter threshold (pick value must be strictly less than 4th-lowest, not 3rd-lowest). This targets the LeBron REB T4 miss mechanism — HOU's elite offensive rebounding structurally reduces total rebound pool for LAL players regardless of minutes or historical patterns.
3. For Evan Mobley AST T2 (and similar big-man AST picks where dossier CAUTION is formally stated for T4+ but T2 is treated as 'safe'): require competitive_split ≥ 80% at T2 for big men with documented cross-season AST suppression in playoffs. Mobley's competitive split was 86%, which passes, but his H2H 7/7 overrode the structural suppression concern. Going forward, when dossier CAUTION language exists for any tier, apply a –5% dossier-awareness penalty even at tiers nominally below the caution threshold, and require the final confidence to clear 78% rather than 70% to proceed.
4. For series game 5+ picks involving players with the LATE_FADER modifier (e.g. Harden PTS, Mobley PTS), ensure the H27/H31 G5-7 fade penalty is applied cumulatively — the current tier_walk reasoning applied LATE_FADER and H27 penalties but the final emitted confidence (68% for Mobley PTS, 68% for Harden PTS) still ran. Consider a hard rule: if post-penalty confidence falls to 68–70% AND the player has both LATE_FADER and VOLATILE tags, skip the pick rather than emit at the minimum floor. Both picks hit, but the risk-reward on marginal floor picks with multiple structural warnings does not justify inclusion.
5. The Ingram injury_exit reinforces the need to treat FG_COLD + VOLATILE + minimum-confidence (70%) PTS picks on road underdogs as 'skip-eligible' even when the H2H and series data nominally qualify the pick. Ingram's pick had: FG_COLD –17% (mandatory step), VOLATILE, 70% confidence, road underdog, severe playoff FG suppression (–6.9% per dossier), and series avg of only 14.8 PPG. Any player combining 3+ of these risk factors at the minimum confidence floor should be flagged for manual review or automatic skip — the pick qualified on paper but had the thinnest possible margin for error, and an early injury exit made it a miss. While injury_event misses do not fault the analyst, the risk profile made this the pick most vulnerable to any negative game-event.
