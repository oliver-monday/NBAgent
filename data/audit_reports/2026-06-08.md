## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Upgrade the KAT H27 STRONG CAUTION directive from 'directional signal' to 'mandatory tier step-down' in NBA Finals context. When H27 shows STRONG CAUTION on PTS (not merely caution) and the player is on the losing side of a CLOSE or COMPETITIVE game script, the LATE_RISER offset should not be permitted to restore the original tier. Specifically: H27 STRONG CAUTION + FG_MARGIN_NEG → execute two full tier steps (T20→T15→T10) rather than one, with no partial offset from LATE_RISER. This would have moved KAT to T10, which he hit (11 actual ≥ 10).
2. Establish a 'persistent role instability' flag for secondary scorers who have logged ≥2 games under 25 minutes in the current series (regardless of season baseline). When this flag is active AND the ATL-series (or multi-series cross-check) T10 hit rate is below 60%, treat PTS T10 as a skip regardless of the current-series H2H record. Bridges' ATL series 3/6 T10 combined with documented coaching minutes compression across G2-G4 of the SAS series should have triggered this. The current-series H2H recency bias overrode a legitimate structural pattern.
3. Apply a 'low-volume AST regression cap' for secondary players (raw_avgs AST < 4.0) at T2: when the hit rate is ≥9/10 in H2H or series data but raw_avgs AST < 4.0, cap confidence at 72% rather than 80%, regardless of the consistent tag. The 95%→80% path for Vassell AST T2 remained too high for a player where 0-assist games are a normal distribution outcome. A hard 72% cap on secondary-player AST T2 (raw_avgs < 4.0) would reflect the true variance risk of low-volume AST props more accurately.
4. When a SAS away player has both FG_COLD and trend=down on PTS and the opponent is NYK (confirmed tough defense), require the combined penalty to execute a mandatory confidence floor reduction to 70% maximum, even after the tier step. Vassell PTS hit at 70% validates the floor conservatism here — the -10pp vs_tough degradation penalty was the correct mechanism and should be formalized as a rule: 'SAS road + FG_COLD + trend=down + tough opponent → cap at 70% after step, no exceptions for cross-series records.' This rule was applied correctly for Vassell PTS and should be locked in.
5. The 76-80% confidence band underperformed (66.7% actual vs 78.0% expected, 2/3 hits) driven by the Vassell AST T2 miss. Before assigning confidence in the 76-80% band to AST props with raw_avgs < 4.0, require one of: (a) iron_floor=true confirmed, (b) opponent AST DvP rated 'soft,' or (c) consistent tag with cross-series 10/10 record across ≥3 different series. Vassell had the consistent tag and 10/10 but only 2 series, and the road Finals context introduced compression risk that the 80% confidence did not adequately reflect. The 76-80% band is currently the weakest-calibrated band and needs tighter gates for low-volume props.
