## Lessons
- VJ Edgecombe AST T2 (iron_floor, 80% confidence) missed with 1 assist in 42 minutes in a COMPETITIVE game (PHI won by 12). The iron_floor tag did not protect against a game where Edgecombe's role was purely scorer/rebounder with no playmaking opportunity generated. The post-game web recap confirms no external factor — he simply did not create assist opportunities despite full minutes. Iron_floor on AST props for non-primary-playmakers (secondary scorers and wings) is structurally fragile: minutes guarantee does not translate to assist accumulation when a player's game-to-game role concentrates on scoring rather than creation. The 8/10 historical hit rate reflects games where Edgecombe happened to contribute playmaking; in a play-in game with elevated scoring focus and specific matchup roles, the playmaking floor was absent even at T2.
- AST iron_floor props on secondary scorers and wing players warrant a role-purity check before application: the iron_floor tag is most durable for primary playmakers (PGs with raw_avgs AST ≥ 5.0). For wings and secondary scorers, a game where the player's usage concentrates on scoring can produce 0-1 assists regardless of minutes. The system should flag AST iron_floor picks where the player's primary role is scoring (PTS raw_avg >> AST raw_avg) as carrying structural miss risk even at T2.
- The 76-80% confidence band produced only 1 hit in 2 picks (50.0% actual vs 78.0% expected), entirely driven by the Edgecombe AST miss at 80%. Sample size is too small (n=2) to draw firm conclusions, but the AST-specific risk at this band — specifically iron_floor AST on non-primary-playmakers — warrants monitoring across future sessions.
- Play-in single-elimination context dramatically elevated actual scoring outputs relative to conservative tier-walked picks: Bane scored 34 on a T10 pick, Garland scored 21 on a T10 pick, Curry scored 35 on a T15 pick, Maxey scored 31 on a T20 pick. This confirms that min_floor and VOLATILE step-downs are correctly applied as conservative floors but the system may be systematically underconfident in high-leverage single-elimination games where players maximize effort and minutes. A play-in minutes override rule (relaxing min_floor caps by one step) deserves formal codification given the consistent pattern.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Codify a play-in / single-elimination minutes override rule: When the game context is confirmed play-in or playoff Game 7 (single-elimination equivalent), relax the min_floor<24 PTS confidence cap from 84% to 87% and do not apply the mandatory min_floor step-down when proj_min ≥ 34. Evidence: Bane (min_floor 17.9, played 40), Garland (min_floor 23.4, played 31), Curry (min_floor 25.0, played 36), Maxey (min_floor n/a, played 42) — all played well above their regular-season floors in elimination contexts. The current rule systematically over-penalizes min_floor in games where benching is structurally unavailable.
2. Add a role-purity gatecheck for AST iron_floor picks on non-primary-playmakers: Before applying iron_floor to an AST prop, check whether the player's raw_avgs PTS is materially higher than raw_avgs AST (e.g. PTS > 3× AST). If so, the iron_floor tag should be downgraded to 'soft_floor' and the pick confidence capped 5pp lower (e.g. 80% → 75%). Apply this specifically to wings, forwards, and secondary scorers. Primary playmakers (PG with raw_avgs AST ≥ 5.0) retain full iron_floor protection. This targets the Edgecombe AST T2 miss pattern directly.
3. Apply PHI suppressor treatment to opponent props more consistently in play-in/playoff contexts: The PHI system-wide suppressor (65.0% hit rate, −22.1pp) was noted in the Season Context but did not appear to reduce confidence on ORL player props in this session. Bane and Edgecombe props vs PHI should have had PHI suppressor treatment applied (prefer one tier lower or require 9+/10 hit rate). Bane hit big and Edgecombe's AST missed — the suppressor was directionally correct on the AST miss. Add a rule: when opp = PHI, apply suppressor treatment to ALL prop types including AST, capping confidence at 75% unless hit_rate_display = 9/10.
4. For VOLATILE AST props at T4 (non-iron-floor), require a positive or stable trend before selection: Both Garland AST T4 (trend=down, hit) and Maxey AST T4 (trend=down, hit) cleared, but the Edgecombe AST miss reinforces that VOLATILE AST props with trend=down carry genuine miss risk that the current −5% VOLATILE deduction alone may not fully price. Add a soft rule: VOLATILE AST at T4+ with trend=down and no iron_floor → require hit_rate_display ≥ 8/10 to proceed; at exactly 7/10 with trend=down, skip regardless of iron_floor status.
5. Validate the iron_floor+VOLATILE incompatibility override for 3PM more thoroughly in playoffs: Curry's 3PM T3 hit (7 threes) with the iron_floor stripped by VOLATILE incompatibility was a clean validation. However, in future sessions where a player has confirmed minutes restrictions (avg_minutes ≤ 28) AND VOLATILE AND iron_floor+VOLATILE incompatibility fires, add a secondary check: if avg_minutes ≤ 28, step down one additional tier (T3 → T2) before evaluating, since per-minute volume is compressed. Do not apply this in play-in/elimination contexts where the override rule from recommendation #1 already elevates projected minutes.
