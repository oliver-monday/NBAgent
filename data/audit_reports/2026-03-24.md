## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Introduce a hard exclusion rule: if the tier_walk field explicitly labels a pick 'SKIP' in its reasoning, the pick must not appear as an active pick regardless of a post-processor confidence correction. Knueppel PTS T15 was internally classified as SKIP due to blowout_secondary_scorer but still entered the active pick pool — this is a pipeline integrity gap. Add a SKIP-propagation check so any pick whose tier_walk contains 'SKIP' as its final determination is automatically voided before publication.
2. For guard/wing REB props at T4 (the system's minimum REB tier), require the player's raw REB average to be ≥5.0 before qualifying at 78% confidence. Harden's REB average as a guard-role player is structurally below center-level production — a T4 pick at the exact 78% floor leaves no buffer when close game scripts keep him in creation mode rather than rebounding mode. Apply a minimum raw_avg_reb ≥ 5.0 gate or step to a lower confidence acknowledgment (70%) for guards at T4.
3. When Mitchell and Harden both play, use the standard (with-both) AST averages rather than the without-Harden averages as the baseline for Mitchell AST picks. The without-Harden data (5.9 APG) is not operative when Harden is active and handling primary facilitation — the co-creator dynamic compresses Mitchell's AST toward 3–4 in a full-minutes Harden game. Add a co-creator AST compression flag: when two players sharing the floor both have raw_avgs_AST ≥ 4.0, apply an additional -5% confidence to the secondary playmaker's AST props at T4+.
4. For CLOSE game scripts (margin < 10 as established pre-game by spread_abs < 8 or confirmed post-game), apply an additional -5% penalty to guard AST props at T4+ against tough defenses. Mitchell AST T4 vs ORL (tough) in a spread_abs=10.5 game that ended at 5-point margin exemplifies the risk: tough defenses remain fully engaged in close games and suppress creation sequences throughout, unlike blowouts where the defense may soften late. The current model accounts for blowout risk but does not specifically penalize tight-spread AST picks vs. tough defenses.
5. Validate the 76-80% confidence band calibration — this session's 76-80% band hit rate was 66.7% (2/3) against an expected 78.0%, with both misses (Harden REB at 78%, Mitchell AST at 74% rounded up) falling in this range. The band is underperforming expected rate. In future sessions, picks in the 76-80% band should be treated with extra scrutiny equivalent to one tier step down, or the analyst should require a higher base hit rate (9/10 rather than 8/10) before assigning confidence in this band to avoid systematic over-placement in a calibration-weak zone.
