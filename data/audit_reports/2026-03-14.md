## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Add a veteran load-management minutes-cap signal for known minutes-managed players (Kawhi Leonard being the prototype): if a player's season avg_minutes is below 30 and they are age 33+, apply an additional −10% confidence penalty on all props and flag as 'load_mgmt_risk'. Kawhi's 26 minutes played confirms the system had no mechanism to anticipate this beyond the B2B flag, which was inapplicable here.
2. Elevate the SAS floor-compression watch item to a conditional hard gate for high-threshold props: for PTS T15+ and AST T4+ against San Antonio, require hit_rate ≥ 9/10 AND trend ≠ down to proceed. The pattern is now confirmed across Brandon Miller PTS (−9 miss), LaMelo Ball AST (−2 miss), and prior session misses — the n=4 threshold justifies a provisional hard gate ahead of the ≥400-pick reconfirmation target.
3. Enforce a skip condition for VOLATILE + min_floor < 20 + away game combinations on PTS props: Jamal Murray's T15 miss (5 actual, 36 min played, no external cause) demonstrates that the step-down rule alone is insufficient when all three risk factors co-occur. The correct action when VOLATILE + min_floor < 20 + away = skip entirely, not step down. Apply retroactively as a rule update for the next session.
4. Implement a workflow enforcement rule: any pick whose tier_walk reasoning field concludes with a 'skip' determination must be excluded from the submission queue. Payton Pritchard PTS T10 was analytically identified as a skip (VOLATILE -5% → 65%, below threshold) but was submitted and missed (4 actual). The analytical logic was correct; the execution was the failure. Add a post-processing gate that screens reasoning fields for skip language before finalizing the pick list.
5. For multi-prop stacks on players in CHA/SA games, cap at two legs per player and require at least one leg to be a secondary stat (AST or REB, not PTS) with iron_floor present: Brandon Miller's PTS miss (6 actual) could not be offset because the iron_floor only protected AST and 3PM. When the SAS scheme flag is active, PTS should be the leg most likely to be suppressed — structure stacks to hedge PTS exposure with lower-variance secondary props.
