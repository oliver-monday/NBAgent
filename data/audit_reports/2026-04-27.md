## Lessons
- AST picks for non-primary creators are the slate's only failure category (2/4 hit rate): both misses were AST picks — McDaniels AST T2 (actual 1) and Cunningham AST T8 (actual 6). The system's AST pick selection logic is systematically overconfident relative to game-script and role reality. The 50% AST hit rate versus 100% across PTS, REB, and 3PM is a stark signal that AST picks require additional structural gatekeeping.
- Cunningham AST T8 miss: the pick relied on H2H 5/6 at T8 and series avg 8.0 AST, but the series avg was inflated by regular-season H2H data. The post-game news confirms 8 turnovers were the causal mechanism — ORL's defensive pressure generated turnovers that directly cannibalized would-be assist opportunities. The opp_today=tough flag was present and a -5% penalty was applied, but the tough defense penalty was insufficient: a tough defense that generates high turnover volume specifically suppresses AST by eliminating the ball-handler's ability to complete creation sequences. The tier_walk correctly identified series 2/3 at T8 as a flag but proceeded to T8 anyway — this series underperformance (2/3 = 67%) should have triggered a mandatory step to T6, not just a confidence reduction.
- McDaniels AST T2 miss: the pick cited 4/4 series hits and Edwards OUT for elevated ball-handling responsibility — but the post-game news confirms McDaniels played only 27 minutes under a diagnosed left knee patella tendinopathy and bone bruise. The injury was documented in the pre-series injury landscape ('left knee patella tendinopathy + bone bruise, G3: full-load breakout, ramp-up confirmed complete') but the G4 diary entry noted '12/8/3 (4-11, 33 min) — steady contributor with no restriction observed.' The post-game context reveals a restriction was present in G5 that was not flagged pre-game. The human_verdict was manual_skip for exactly this reason — the trim_reasons correctly identified the raw_avg of 2.4 against a T2 threshold as leaving 'virtually no margin' and noted non-primary creator AST suppression in a losing game script. The pick ran despite human flagging; the human assessment was correct.
- The DET×AST suppressor fired correctly for Cunningham even as a primary playmaker: the series diary had already documented Cunningham's G4 17-pt AST miss context (opp_today=tough) and the suppressor note states 'primary playmakers (raw_avgs AST ≥ 6.0) may still be less affected' — but the word 'may' is doing too much work. In a competitive CLOSE game (ORL won by 6), ORL's defensive structure specifically targeted Cunningham's creation sequences, generating 8 turnovers. The suppressor should not be waived entirely for primary creators in tough-defense contexts; a -5pp penalty beyond the tough-defense deduction should apply when series data shows 2/3 at the target tier.
- Both AST misses occurred in CLOSE games (DET-ORL margin 6, both teams competitive throughout). In close playoff games, AST production is more volatile than in blowouts or competitive games because offensive possessions are more contested, shot quality matters more, and ball-handlers face heavier defensive attention on creation sequences. The system should apply an additional -5pp AST penalty in series where opp_today=tough AND the pre-game spread is within 5 points (close game likely), regardless of primary/secondary creator classification.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Add a series-underperformance mandatory step rule for AST picks: if a player's series hit rate at the target AST tier is below 70% (e.g., Cunningham 2/3 = 67% at T8), treat this as a mandatory step-down signal equivalent to FG_COLD — step to the next lower tier rather than absorbing it as a confidence deduction. This would have moved Cunningham from T8 to T6, where the structural floor was more defensible.
2. Tighten the tough-defense AST penalty: the current -5% for opp_today=tough on AST picks is insufficient when the opponent also generates high turnover volume (opp_today=tough + turnover-heavy defensive scheme). For primary playmakers, apply -10pp AST penalty (not -5pp) when the opponent is flagged tough AND the series diary documents defensive pressure generating turnovers. ORL vs DET is the prototype: the G4 diary explicitly noted 8 turnovers limiting playmaking.
3. Do not select AST T2 for non-primary creators (raw_avgs AST < 3.5) when (a) the player has a documented injury concern in the injury landscape and (b) the player is on the road as an underdog by more than 7 points. McDaniels met all three criteria: raw_avg AST 2.4, knee concern documented, MIN road underdog by 11.5. The human manual_skip was correct; automate this as a hard skip gate.
4. Apply a CLOSE-game AST volatility penalty: in playoff series where the pre-game spread is ≤5 points (game projected as close), apply an additional -5pp to all AST picks for both primary and secondary creators above T4. Close games concentrate defensive attention on ball-handlers, compress creation sequences, and produce more contested possessions — the two AST misses today (Cunningham T8, McDaniels T2) both occurred in the CLOSE game (ORL-DET, margin 6). This penalty would have pushed Cunningham below the 68% AST floor at T8 and prevented the pick.
5. When the human_verdict is manual_skip or trim on an AST pick with a raw_avg within 0.5 units of the pick_value threshold (e.g., McDaniels raw_avg 2.4 vs T2), do not run the pick. The human auditor correctly identified margin fragility for McDaniels AST T2 — the trim_reason 'raw_avg of only 2.4 against a T2 threshold leaves virtually no margin' is precisely the right gatekeeping logic. Codify this as a system rule: if raw_avg < pick_value + 0.5 on any AST pick AND human_verdict = manual_skip, void the pick automatically rather than running it at minimum confidence.
