## Recommendations for Analyst
1. BLOWOUT LOSING-SIDE PTS FLOOR COMPRESSION RULE: When the actual game margin reaches BLOWOUT territory (≥20 points) and a player is on the LOSING side, T20 PTS props — even structural locks like SGA — are not immune to compression in the final quarter when the deficit makes scoring inefficient. The system's BLOWOUT_RISK gate fires only at pre-game spread_abs thresholds, but any game can become a blowout regardless of the opening line. For road games in a series where the pre-game spread is within 5 points (competitive spread), add a note in the tier_walk that T20 on the road carries a non-trivial blowout-loss tail risk. This does not change selection but should inform when the T20 lock directive is treated as 'near-certain' vs 'very high but not immune' — specifically in road playoff games where blowout losses are feasible.
2. WALK-REVERT BLOWOUT EXPOSURE: The SGA PTS pick reverted from T15 to T20 because no T15 market existed. When a walk-revert occurs (step-down reversed due to no market), apply an additional -3% confidence penalty in lieu of the structural cushion the step-down would have provided. The current rule applies only -2pp. In a WCF road game context, the missing cushion from the revert was material — the -2pp penalty proved insufficient to price in the tail risk. Upgrade the walk-revert no-market penalty from -2pp to -3pp for playoff road games.
3. MINIMUM-TIER PTS TRAP WITH BLOWOUT WINNING SIDE: Harper PTS T10 was the minimum viable tier with no downward path and a confirmed 21-minute blowout-managed appearance. The rule should explicitly state: when a non-primary, non-star player (raw_avgs PTS < 18) is on the WINNING side of a series with a pre-game spread ≤ 5 points, and the only available PTS tier is the minimum (T10), add a skip-eligible flag if (a) FG_MARGIN_THIN fires AND (b) competitive split is below 65% AND (c) WCF/ECF within-series hit rate at the selected tier is below 70%. All three conditions fired for Harper and the pick ran at 70% confidence — the combination should have produced a skip recommendation. Codify this triple-condition minimum-tier skip rule for non-primary scorers in playoff rounds 3+.
4. 3PM BLOWOUT LOSING-SIDE SUPPRESSION: SGA 3PM T1 scored 0 in a game OKC lost by 21 on the road. The VOLATILE tag and within-series 2/6 at T2 were both present. For any 3PM pick on the LOSING side of what becomes a blowout, the non-iron_floor 80% ceiling rule understates the true risk — offensive isolation in a trailing context often compresses three-point attempts in favor of paint attacks. When a 3PM pick has VOLATILE tag + within-series failure rate above 40% at the adjacent tier, downgrade the confidence ceiling from 80% to 75% for that pick. This would have moved SGA 3PM from 78% to a value below the 76% minimum for non-iron_floor 3PM in WCF context.
5. MINUTES-MANAGED ROOKIE IN BLOWOUT WIN CONTEXT: Harper's 21-minute appearance in a 21-point SAS win is consistent with a coaching decision to limit a developing player's blowout minutes rather than any injury or tactical adjustment. The system should add a blowout-winning-side minutes compression check for non-star players (raw_avgs PTS < 18, proj_min < 25): when SAS (or any team with a dominant blowout record) wins by a large margin, reserve players are systematically pulled. Apply a -5pp additional penalty to any non-primary SAS scorer with proj_min < 25 at the T10 minimum tier in WCF/ECF games — this specifically targets the Harper-type profile where no tier step exists but minute compression is structurally probable in a heavy favorite blowout win scenario.
