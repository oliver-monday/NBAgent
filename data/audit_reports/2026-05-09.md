## Recommendations for Analyst
1. For REB picks where competitive hit rate drops materially below 70% (e.g. competitive=66% as seen with Duren REB), apply a mandatory additional -8pp penalty rather than treating it as a soft adjustment. If the resulting confidence falls below 72%, step the tier down one level regardless of series form. Series form (2/2) should not fully offset a structural competitive-game suppression signal — the two signals address different sample populations and the competitive sample is more directly relevant to playoff game scripts.
2. When a REB pick's 25th-percentile gate analysis finds the 3rd-lowest recent game value is close to or at the pick threshold (e.g. PHX series avg 9.4 with estimated low games near 7-8), and the competitive hit rate is simultaneously below 70%, treat this as a compound gate signal and step down one additional tier even if the 25th-pct gate nominally clears. The Duren T8 miss with competitive=66% and a PHX series that had borderline 3rd-lowest values illustrates that narrow gate clearances combined with poor competitive rates create outsized tail risk.
3. The obs_walk 'declined' outcome on Duren REB (T10→T8 declined, no T5 market) identified a structural limitation: when no lower market tier exists and FG_MARGIN_THIN fires, the analyst correctly held with a confidence penalty, but the combination of FG_MARGIN_THIN + FG_COLD (-21%) + competitive=66% on a REB pick with no walk-down option available should trigger a skip consideration rather than a held pick at floor confidence. Codify: if FG_COLD ≥15% AND competitive<70% AND no lower market tier exists for a REB pick, classify as skip-eligible at the analyst's discretion.
4. The Harden PTS dossier advisory ('AVOID PTS in playoffs') was correctly applied as a -10pp penalty rather than a hard skip, and the pick hit (19 actual vs T10). This validates the current treatment — dossier AVOID signals on PTS should continue to be operationalized as confidence penalties at conservative tiers (T10 and below) rather than absolute skips when series evidence (2/2 T10) and H2H data (3/3 vs DET) provide strong override context. Do not escalate the AVOID directive to a hard skip rule based on this slate.
5. The walk-revert outcome on Harden AST (T2 market unavailable, reverted to T4 with -3pp penalty) produced a 7-actual vs T4 hit. When a walk-revert occurs due to market unavailability, the -3pp penalty-in-lieu approach is functioning correctly — no rule change needed. Reinforce that walk-revert picks should continue to apply the -3pp penalty and remain in the slate, as the reversion to a higher tier with a small confidence haircut correctly reflects the incremental risk of not fully walking down.
