## Lessons
- LeBron PTS missed at 12 vs. a T15 floor with a 9/10 hit rate (95%) — the root cause is the Without-Luka baseline (6-game sample, n=6) being used when Luka was QUES not confirmed OUT. Post-game news confirms Luka was OUT (confirmed absence listed in YESTERDAY'S NOTABLE ABSENCES), meaning the baseline was structurally valid, but the actual output of 12 PTS (34 min) still missed a T15 floor in a 1-point game (LAL 105, ORL 104). In an extremely close game that came down to the final possession, LeBron's scoring volume was likely managed and minutes-efficient rather than volume-heavy — the CLOSE game script (margin=1) did not suppress minutes but may have compressed possessions in late-game defense-first scenarios. The 9/10 historical hit rate is sound, but the combination of (a) a single-digit-possession game script, (b) a 6-game Without-Luka baseline sample (small), and (c) a tough ORL defense rating on PTS (opp=mid, not soft) represents a convergence of marginal risks the model treated independently. A tighter rule requiring confirmed OUT (not just QUES) before applying the Without-Star PTS baseline would have either voided this pick or reduced confidence below the 80% threshold.
- Kawhi Leonard REB missed at 3 vs. a pick of 4 with a 9/10 hit rate and iron-floor=true at T4 — this is a variance miss given the tier and hit rate, but the trend=down signal on REB was present and the analyst applied only a −5% confidence penalty rather than considering whether a rebounding pick in a competitive-to-blowout game (LAC won by 7, a CLOSE game) warranted greater scrutiny. The tier_walk notes a T4 exact-match exemption but the Without-Garland baseline at T4=55% (below threshold) was flagged and the standard T4 was used instead — this is the correct path per rules, but it means the qualifying tier was the standard baseline, not a verified Without-Garland line. REB at the minimum valid tier (T4) with trend=down and a QUES designation is a structurally thin pick even with iron-floor status.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Require confirmed OUT (not QUESTIONABLE) before applying any Without-Star PTS baseline to a pick. If the star is QUES at pick time, default to the shared-lineup baseline and reduce the tier one step; do not build a T15 or higher PTS floor on a 6-game Without-X sample when X may play. This would have avoided the LeBron PTS miss and reduces baseline-contamination risk across all star-absence props.
2. Apply a minimum-sample guard on Without-Star baselines: if the Without-X sample is fewer than 10 games, treat it as supplementary context only — do not use it as the primary tier qualifier. Use the standard baseline tier and note the small-sample Without-Star data as a supporting signal at most.
3. For REB props at the minimum valid tier (T4 exact-match exemption) with trend=down and a QUES injury tag, require at least two of the following to proceed: iron_floor=true, opp=soft, spread_abs<8, and no VOLATILE tag. Kawhi's REB pick had only iron_floor and opp=soft — trend=down and QUES should together constitute a skip condition at the minimum tier.
4. Introduce a CLOSE game script discount for high-volume counting stat PTS picks: when the pre-game spread is ≤4 points, apply a −3% confidence penalty to PTS picks at T15 or higher because close games increase the probability of late-game defensive possessions and intentional-foul scenarios that suppress individual scoring volume without reducing minutes. The LAL-ORL game (spread 3.5, final margin 1) is the prototype case.
5. Do not combine trend=down with a Without-Star baseline on the same pick without explicitly flagging the double-dependency risk in the tier_walk. For LeBron AST (HIT, 4.0 actual vs. 4 pick — exact threshold), the trend=down signal nearly caused a miss; for LeBron PTS it contributed to one. Tag any pick where both trend=down and a Without-X baseline are active as DOUBLE_DEPENDENCY and cap confidence at 76% maximum.
