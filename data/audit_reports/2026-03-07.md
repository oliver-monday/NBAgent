## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a hard rule: when a player's 3PM trend is 'down' AND minutes are projected at or below 30 (e.g. Kawhi's 28 minutes), do not select even T1 3PM regardless of historical hit rate. The T1 floor for 3PM is not absolute — it depends on the player attempting at least one three, which is not guaranteed with managed minutes and a down shot-selection trend. Apply a 'down trend + minutes cap' flag that blocks 3PM picks below a 9/10 hit rate threshold.
2. For AST picks at T4 or above on big-man/forward players (Banchero type), require the opponent's AST DvP to be 'soft' — not just 'neutral' or 'mid' — before clearing the pick. When MIN's defensive profile explicitly suppresses big-man usage and assists, and the player's own T4 AST hit rate is only 75%, a tough DvP should be a hard block, not just a -5% adjustment. The current penalty system is insufficient for AST tier ≥4 against elite switching defenses.
3. Introduce a 'down trend + tough DvP + borderline tier' triple-flag that prevents PTS picks at 70% confidence when all three conditions are simultaneously present. Randle's T15 pick had 7/10 hit rate (borderline), down trend, and tough DvP — any two of these alone might be acceptable, but all three together should produce a skip rather than a reduced-confidence selection. This rule would have correctly skipped Randle PTS and potentially saved a parlay leg.
4. The data shows a systematic overperformance in the 70-75% confidence band (80.0% actual vs 72.5% expected) and the 76-80% band (92.3% actual vs 78.0% expected). While this is encouraging, it suggests the model may be systematically underconfident — consider whether VOLATILE and BLOWOUT_RISK deductions are being double-applied or over-weighted, suppressing confidence scores that should be higher. Review whether VOLATILE -5% and BLOWOUT_RISK -5% are additive caps or independent adjustments, and ensure they are not stacking unnecessarily.
5. For Cade Cunningham, the stats context showed opponent as MIA (not BKN as listed in the pick), suggesting a data mismatch between the pick's stated opponent and the player stats context. Before finalizing picks, implement a pre-submission opponent cross-check that flags any discrepancy between the pick object's 'opponent' field and the stats context 'opponent' field — this may have contributed to the NO_DATA outcome if the game was misidentified or not played.
