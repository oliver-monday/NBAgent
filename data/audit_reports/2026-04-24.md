## Lessons
- REB is the structural weakness of this slate: 3 misses came from 3 different REB picks (Paul George REB T4: 0 actual, Jayson Tatum REB T8: 5 actual, Stephon Castle REB T4: 2 actual). All three players had non-zero minutes (41, 42, 34 respectively) and were not injury-limited. REB outcomes are more volatile game-to-game than PTS or AST, and the 57.1% REB hit rate on this slate (4/7) should prompt structural review of REB tier selection discipline.
- Tatum REB T8 miss reveals that the 'vs_tough=60%' and 'competitive=57%' signals at T8 were meaningful warnings that were insufficiently weighted. The tier_walk acknowledged these as 'concerning' but still emitted at 72% exactly at the REB minimum floor — this is the weakest possible confidence for a pick and should trigger a skip or lower-tier evaluation. When REB vs_tough and competitive rates are both below 65% at the selected tier, the pick is borderline at best.
- Paul George REB T4 with raw_avg_REB of 3.8 (below the T4 threshold) and actual output of 0 rebounds in 41 minutes is a clear signal: picking REB from below the player's average creates thin distributional support. The trim note flagged this correctly ('raw_avg_REB of 3.8 is below the T4 threshold') but the pick still ran. When raw_avg is below pick_value, the pick is already in the tail of the distribution — REB props should require raw_avg strictly above pick_value, not at or below it.
- Castle REB T4 miss (2 actual in 34 minutes) occurred in a COMPETITIVE game (SAS won by 12) — not a blowout, not a late-game coasting situation. The VOLATILE tag on Castle REB was applied (-3%) but the underlying 80% base was driven by only 2 series games. Small series samples (n=2) should not anchor REB confidence at 80% when the VOLATILE tag is also present; the combination should produce a more significant reduction or a skip.
- The human_verdict='trim' flags on REB picks (George, Tatum, Queta) correctly identified the structural fragility of these picks. The trim rationale on George REB ('raw_avg_REB of 3.8 is below the T4 threshold, post_miss_hr for REB is only 50%') and Tatum REB ('VOLATILE on REB, PHI def_recency tightened') were accurate pre-game warnings. The system's trim/manual_skip verdicts are better-calibrated than the base picks on REB props — when the human reviewer trims a REB pick, that signal should be treated as a near-skip.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a hard rule: REB picks require raw_avg_REB strictly greater than pick_value (not equal to or below). Paul George's raw_avg of 3.8 below the T4 threshold and Castle's thin distributional support at T4 are prototype cases. When raw_avg_REB < pick_value, classify as selection_error risk and skip unless iron_floor is confirmed.
2. When REB vs_tough AND competitive rates are both below 65% at the selected tier, require either (a) a tier step-down, or (b) 9/10 hit rate at the selected tier before emitting. Tatum's REB T8 had both signals flagged in the tier_walk as 'concerning' — the 72% minimum floor emission should not be allowed when two independent context rates both signal sub-65%. Treat dual-signal degradation as a skip trigger, not a 72% floor.
3. Apply a small-series-sample penalty to REB confidence when n_series ≤ 2 and VOLATILE is tagged. Castle's REB T4 was anchored on 2 series games at 80% base with a VOLATILE tag — this combination should cap at 68% maximum (below the 72% REB floor), effectively triggering a skip. Series samples of 2 are insufficient to override VOLATILE-tagged REB props at minimum tiers.
4. Treat human_verdict='trim' on REB picks as a de facto confidence ceiling of 70% for audit purposes. Across this slate, every trimmed REB pick either missed or hit marginally (Queta hit with 7 actual but was correctly identified as minutes-fragile). Establish a workflow rule: if the pick qualifies for trim based on the trim_reasons criteria (raw_avg below threshold, VOLATILE + blowout risk, min_floor near-zero), cap the emitted confidence at 70% and flag for manual review before including in parlays.
5. For the BOS vs PHI series specifically: do not fade BOS players purely on the PHI suppressor when opp_defense is rated 'mid' and the series H2H already shows 2/2 hits at the selected tier. The suppressor is real in competitive games but the within-series hit rate data should be weighted more heavily than the full-season suppressor signal after G2+. Continue applying opp_today=mid as the operative signal when series-level data contradicts the full-season suppressor, and reserve the suppressor penalty for first-game picks in the series where no within-series data exists.
