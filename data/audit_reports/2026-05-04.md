## Recommendations for Analyst
1. IMPLEMENT LOSING-SIDE BLOWOUT ASYMMETRY CAP FOR HIGH-CONFIDENCE PICKS: When a player is on the road-underdog side AND spread_abs is 6-10 (moderate spread, below the formal 11-point blowout ceiling trigger), apply a mandatory 10pp confidence reduction on PTS picks at 80%+ confidence. This targets the Maxey PTS T20 pattern (88% confidence, +7.5 underdog, 39-point actual blowout). The current rule correctly applies blowout penalties at spread_abs > 11 but leaves the 6-10 range unpenalized for high-confidence picks. A road underdog at +7.5 in a playoff series (where score variance is higher) has meaningful blowout-loss tail risk that iron_floor status should not fully absorb.
2. ADD EXPLICIT BLOWOUT-WINNER AST COMPRESSION RULE: When a player is on the favored/winning side AND spread_abs > 7 in a playoff game, apply a -5pp confidence penalty to AST picks at T4+. The current framework applies blowout ceiling caps only to PTS at spread_abs > 11, but blowout winners accumulate assists at a compressed rate when stars are benched in the second half. The Brunson AST T4 miss (3 assists in 31 minutes of a 39-point win) directly illustrates this gap. The rule should fire for all AST T4+ picks on the favored team when spread_abs > 7 in playoff games.
3. REQUIRE NEAR-SKIP FOR REB PROPS WHEN VS_TOUGH RATE IS BELOW 55%: Anunoby's vs_tough=44% at T4 REB was visible in the quant and the pick ran anyway at 72% confidence. When the prop-type-specific vs_tough rate falls below 55% and the opponent defense is rated 'tough', the system should treat this as a de-facto near-skip condition (require 9/10 hit rate at the primary tier OR step down one tier further, even at T4 where the 25th-percentile gate is suspended). The T4 exception from the 25th-percentile gate should not exempt picks from the vs_tough degradation signal — these are independent mechanisms.
4. APPLY ENHANCED CAUTION TO EMBIID POST-SURGERY PROPS IN ROAD PLAYOFF GAMES: The Embiid PTS T20 and REB T6 both missed in G1 of a new series, 24 days post-appendectomy, as a road underdog. The conditioning arc (improving but non-linear across G4-G7 of the prior series) combined with road underdog blowout risk and a new opponent defensive scheme creates a compound risk profile that the current tier-walk does not fully capture. Recommended rule: for any player in confirmed post-surgery return status (flagged in SEASON CONTEXT or series diary) playing a road Game 1 of a new series, treat the pick as VOLATILE regardless of tag status and apply the full -3pp playoff VOLATILE deduction, plus require one tier higher than the system's best qualifying tier before selecting.
5. CAP 3PM T1 PICKS FOR PLAYERS WITH TREND=DOWN AND ZERO IRON_FLOOR IN R2 G1 CONTEXTS: Fox 3PM T1 (0 actual, 0-for-4 from three) and Wembanyama 3PM T1 (0 actual, 0-for-8 from three) both missed as R2 G1 openers. Both had trend=down on 3PM and no iron_floor on the prop. The current rule applies a -5pp trend=down penalty at T1 when no iron_floor exists, but the minimum 75% floor still allows the pick to run. For R2 G1 (first game of a new series) when trend=down is active and iron_floor is absent on 3PM, raise the minimum required confidence to 78% before selecting — this would have held both picks below the selection threshold and prevented both misses.
