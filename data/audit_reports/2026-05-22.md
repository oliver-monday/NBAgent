Establish a hard skip rule for QUESTIONABLE soft-tissue injuries (hamstring, calf, soleus, ankle) in WCF/ECF game contexts for players whose injury was documented as an in-game exit in the immediately preceding game (i.e., G2 exit → G3 QUESTIONABLE). The Harper pick correctly applied a -5pp QUESTIONABLE penalty but still ran at 70% — the minimum floor. A player who exited G2 with a soft-tissue injury and is QUESTIONABLE for G3 should be treated as DOUBTFUL-equivalent for pick purposes when the injury was a mid-game exit (not a pre-game designation), resulting in a skip rather than a floor-confidence pick.
4. The 70-75% confidence band clustering in REB props from a single game (two misses: Wemby REB and Holmgren REB, both OKC vs SAS G3) highlights a parlay/concentration risk: when multiple picks at the 70-75% floor share the same game and the same prop type (REB), the effective correlation between outcomes is high. The Analyst should flag when ≥2 REB picks from the same game are both at 70-75% confidence, and require at least one to step down further or be replaced with a higher-confidence alternative. This reduces slate-level exposure to correlated low-confidence REB misses.
5. For Holmgren REB specifically in SAS matchups: the 25th-pct gate correctly fired and walked T6→T4, but T4 still missed by 1 (3 actual). The tier walk was sound, but the pick_value=4 minimum tier was reached in an environment with documented SAS floor compression (-5.0 mean miss margin), Wembanyama's interior presence, and a minutes projection of 28 (which materialized exactly). When the tier walk exhausts to the minimum tier (T4 for REB) AND the game opponent has SAS floor compression active AND the pick involves an interior/combo big man, treat this as a soft skip signal even at T4 — the combination of minimum tier + floor compression environment + elite opposing rebounder produces structural tail risk that the minimum tier exception was not designed to absorb.
