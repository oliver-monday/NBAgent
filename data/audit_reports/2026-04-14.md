1. Add a FG_COLD cross-prop correlation rule: when a player has FG_COLD:-15% active on their PTS prop and the system also generates an AST or 3PM pick for the same player on the same slate, apply a correlated-risk flag to both picks. The FG_COLD state suppresses counting stats across PTS, AST, and 3PM simultaneously — treat these as a correlated bundle, not independent props.
2. Upgrade FG_COLD treatment for secondary-scorer PTS at T10: the current rule treats FG_COLD as informational below T15+. Amend to apply a mandatory 5% confidence deduction (or soft skip recommendation) to any secondary-scorer PTS prop at T10 when FG_COLD ≥15% is active, regardless of tier. The Knueppel T10 miss at 6 PTS on 2/12 FG is the empirical basis — at T10, the cushion is too thin to absorb a full cold-shooting night.
3. In parlay construction, do not combine a FG_COLD-flagged leg with any other leg for the same player on the same night. If Knueppel PTS carries FG_COLD, the Knueppel AST leg should be ineligible as a parlay companion — the efficiency depression is structurally correlated across all counting stats and represents a single-game systemic risk, not two independent outcomes.
