At T1 3PM for a non-perimeter player, a single 0-for-3PM game is always in-range variance, but the signal that Adebayo is a C with relatively low 3PM volume makes this structurally fragile regardless of recent trend. The trend=up tag on 3PM for big men needs additional scrutiny.

However, in a game that finished ORL +5 (CLOSE), Bane played 42 minutes — a usage pattern suggesting heavy offensive load but limited rebounding opportunity at the wing position. The REB miss here reflects that wing/guard REB T2 props are vulnerable to game-flow effects that push the player toward ball-handling rather than crashing the glass. The 9/10 overall rate may overstate reliability in close games where wings operate in transition-heavy modes.
- Jalen Duren PTS T15 missed at 14 actual (one point below threshold): After the mandatory min_floor step T15→T10 was applied in the tier_walk, the pick was published at T10 per the reasoning — yet the graded pick_value shows 15, not 10. This is a pick recording inconsistency: the tier_walk logic stepped down to T10 but the published pick_value remained T15. The actual result of 14 would have been a HIT at T10. ## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Enforce a categorical rule: for C and PF players (Adebayo, Jokic, Duren, Holmgren, etc.), 3PM T1 picks require a minimum of 7/10 hit rate AND the player's 3PM average across L10 must exceed 0.8 per game. A trend=up tag alone is insufficient to qualify a low-volume 3PM prop for a big man — add an explicit L10 3PM average floor gate before publishing these picks.
2. Fix the tier step-down publication pipeline: when a mandatory step-down is applied (e.g. min_floor < 24 triggers T15→T10), the final pick_value field in the published pick must reflect the stepped-down tier (T10 = pick_value: 10), not the original evaluated tier. The Duren PTS case (tier_walk said T10, pick_value published as 15, actual was 14) would have been a HIT at T10. Implement a post-walk pick_value reconciliation check before publication.
3. Add a volatility flag specifically for wing/guard REB T2 props in CLOSE games (spread_abs < 6): when a wing player (SG/SF/PF hybrid) has a REB T2 pick and the pre-game spread is under 6 points, apply an additional -5% confidence deduction. Close games push wings into playmaking roles rather than rebounding, and the Bane REB miss (1 actual, ORL +5 final) illustrates this dynamic. The 9/10 overall rate masks game-script fragility at this tier.
4. Establish a VOLATILE + SKIP enforcement audit: any pick where the tier_walk explicitly concludes 'SKIP' due to VOLATILE-5% dropping below 70% must not appear in the published graded picks above 70% confidence. The Cunningham 3PM T2 pick showed 70% confidence in the graded record despite the tier_walk documenting a SKIP decision. Add a post-processing validation step that cross-checks tier_walk SKIP conclusions against published confidence values and suppresses any pick where SKIP was reached.
5. For 3PM props on players with trend=down, apply the existing hard skip rule consistently even at T1 — the Payton Pritchard 3PM T1 pick was internally flagged as hard-skip (trend=down + tough DvP) but published at 74% and hit. While this was a beneficial outcome, the inconsistency between the tier_walk logic and the published pick creates calibration noise. Either enforce the hard skip universally or document explicitly when iron_floor overrides the skip, so the rule is deterministic rather than ad hoc.
