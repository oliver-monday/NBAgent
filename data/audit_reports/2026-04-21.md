## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a hard ceiling on 3PM T1 OVER picks when market-implied probability exceeds 90%: if the market prices 1+ three-pointers at 93%+, the system's 80%-capped confidence creates a structural FADE, not a selection. Do not select 3PM T1 OVER when market_implied_prob > 90% unless the pick has a positive calibrated edge — use the bet_recommendation tier as a gate, not just confidence_pct.
2. Introduce a multi-injury-concern flag for players returning from injury within 10 games AND managing a concurrent physical issue: Wembanyama qualified (rib contusion April 6, right ankle soreness, only 4 games since return) but was NOT_LISTED at check. For such players, apply a structural -10pp confidence penalty on all props regardless of injury_status_at_check, and do not include them as anchor legs in parlays.
3. When season H2H hit rate vs. a specific series opponent is ≤50% at the selected tier (as with Pritchard PTS T10, 2/4 vs PHI), require the H2H rate to be ≥70% OR the series G1 result to show the tier cleared by ≥5 points before proceeding — a single series game hit does not override a consistent season-long H2H pattern of underperformance against that opponent.
4. For Wembanyama PTS props specifically: the vs_tough=64% at T20 was a material warning signal that the tier-walk noted but absorbed with only a -5pp deduction. In future games, when vs_tough drops >15pp below overall hit rate at the selected tier, treat this as a mandatory one-tier step-down (not just a confidence penalty), yielding T15 at higher confidence rather than T20 at reduced confidence.
5. Strengthen the 3PM parlay construction rule: two 3PM legs from different games (Wemby 3PM + Jabari Smith 3PM) both had market-implied probabilities >70% individually, but combined into a parlay with implied odds of +117 — this represents a low-juice combination where individual cold-shooting nights (especially from a player with a recent injury concern) can collapse the parlay.
