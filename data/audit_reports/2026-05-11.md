## Recommendations for Analyst
1. For Jalen Duren REB specifically: the series T6 rate of 2/3 (67%) fell below the 70% required threshold, but the pick was held at T6 because T4 had no market. When the only available tier has a series hit rate below the 70% minimum floor AND competitive rate below 65%, apply an additional hard skip evaluation: if the series hit rate at the selected tier is < 70% AND no lower market exists, classify as merit_below_floor and skip. The current rule only checks overall season rate against the floor — extend the floor check to also gate on series rate when series sample ≥ 3 games. This would have flagged Duren REB T6 (series 2/3 = 67% < 70%) as a skip candidate.
2. For Jarrett Allen (and similar secondary scorers) against tough defenses: vs_tough=56% is among the lowest matchup-specific rates in the dataset. When a secondary scorer's vs_tough rate falls below 60% at the selected tier AND the player carries a VOLATILE tag, require a mandatory additional step-down of one tier (beyond any FG_MARGIN step already applied). If no lower tier market exists, apply the merit_below_floor skip. This tighter rule specifically targets secondary scorer PTS props where tough defense creates a double suppression that the current -5pp cap does not fully price.
3. For LeBron James AST picks in Game 4+ of a playoff series: the trend=down signal was present and the analyst correctly applied the VOLATILE T6+ penalty. However, the series-based evidence (3/3 at T6) was given too much weight given it was only a 3-game sample in a series where LeBron's minutes and health are actively managed. Add a rule: when a player has a documented within-series injury event (hip, knee, oblique — any non-fatal injury flagged in post-game news from a prior game in the same series), apply an additional -5pp penalty to AST picks at T6+, even if not formally listed as QUESTIONABLE. The post-game news for the Game 2 hip aggravation was available in the series diary context; the afternoon injury agent should be instructed to check series diary injury language as a supplemental injury signal.
4. The 76-80% confidence band performed at 100% (6/6) on this slate, significantly outperforming the 78% expected rate. While one slate is insufficient to recommend raising picks in this band, this reinforces that the current penalty structure (VOLATILE -3pp, FG_MARGIN step-downs, tough defense discounts) is producing well-calibrated outputs in the 76-80% range. Do not tighten minimum confidence thresholds in this band — the current calibration is correct. The 70-75% band (72.7% actual vs. 72.5% expected) is also near-perfectly calibrated; maintain current floors.
5. For any REB prop where the analyst's tier_walk shows competitive rate < 65% at the primary tier AND the 25th-pct gate forces a step-down AND no lower market exists: require the pick to meet a 75% minimum series hit rate at the held tier (not the standard 70% overall floor) before proceeding. Duren REB T6 had series 2/3 (67%) — below a 75% series minimum that would have been a skip signal under this rule. This specifically addresses the scenario where competitive suppression, gate-forced holds, and thin series data combine to create a fragile pick with no downward escape.
