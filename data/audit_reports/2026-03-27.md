However, human auditors flagged 8 of 13 picks as 'trim' or 'manual_skip', meaning the pick card had meaningful structural concerns that happened not to manifest as misses today. The most concentrated risk cluster was the HOU@MEM picks (KD AST T2, Sengun PTS T10, Sengun AST T2) — all three were flagged for blowout + min_floor fragility. - The Sengun PTS T10 pick was flagged 'manual_skip' by the human auditor despite hitting 14 actual vs 10 threshold. The pick was structurally sound on the outcome but the manual_skip classification reflects legitimate pre-game concern: VOLATILE tag + min_floor 21.6 + spread_abs 13.5 is a three-factor fragility stack that the system should consider formalizing as a hard skip rule rather than leaving to human override. If the game had become a true blowout (margin ≥20), Sengun's floor would have been genuinely threatened. Consider adding: if VOLATILE=true AND min_floor < 22 AND spread_abs ≥ 13, skip PTS prop regardless of hit rate.

The system should not recalibrate confidence floors upward based on a single 100% session; the confidence band distribution (all picks clustered in 70-80% range, zero picks in 81%+) reflects appropriate spread_abs cap discipline. Maintaining the cap structure is validated — the caps are doing their job of preventing overconfidence, not suppressing good picks.
