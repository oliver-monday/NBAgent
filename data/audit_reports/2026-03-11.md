## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a live game script adjustment for PTS props on centers and high-usage forwards: when the in-game spread expands beyond 20 points, apply a scoring suppression flag. The Jokic miss (16 PTS in a 36-point blowout) and the reasoning's reliance on pre-game spread (6.5) illustrate that pre-game spread alone is insufficient — build a second-half load management discount into the model for marquee big men in non-competitive games.
2. Add a minutes floor reliability check for away games: Durant's stated min_floor of 32.5 was not met (26 min actual). When a player's away B2B minutes history shows variance >5 min from the stated floor, downgrade confidence by 5% or flag the pick for manual review. The 'min_floor' field should be sourced from away-game-only splits, not overall averages, to avoid overestimating floor reliability on road legs.
3. Tighten the REB floor gate: when the L10 3rd-lowest value equals the pick threshold exactly (as with Sengun REB=6 gating at T6), require a 1-unit cushion — i.e., the 3rd-lowest L10 value must be strictly greater than the pick threshold, not equal to it. An exact match provides no variance buffer and a single outlier game causes a miss. Apply this rule to all prop types.
4. For Jokic PTS specifically: given his triple-double profile and Denver's tendency to run up scores against weak opponents, consider a conditional rule that steps down Jokic PTS by one tier (T25→T20→skip) when the opponent is ranked bottom-5 defensively AND DEN is a home favorite by 6+. The blowout risk is structurally higher for Jokic than for guards because his scoring is more discretionary in garbage time.
5. Validate Kevin Durant's miss classification next session with additional data: his 26-minute appearance against a stated min_floor of 32.5 is either a load management decision (model_gap_signal — no quant field captures coach rest decisions mid-game) or an undisclosed minor injury exit. If confirmed as load management, add a 'star load management away B2B' penalty flag for veterans on road second nights — even those with 100% B2B resilience ratings.
