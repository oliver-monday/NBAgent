## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a chronic carry-over injury penalty for players confirmed to be 'fighting through' a known injury from a prior series that does not appear on the official pre-game injury report. When post-game news from the immediately preceding game references an ankle, knee, or similar structural injury as ongoing, apply a mandatory -5pp confidence reduction to PTS and 3PM props for that player in the next game even if NOT_LISTED on the injury report. This would have caught both Fox PTS T10 and Fox 3PM T1 — the ankle was documented as a carry-over condition and the injury report gap was the structural failure point.
2. For 3PM props on VOLATILE-tagged players with no iron_floor in away playoff elimination games (spread < 8), evaluate whether the pick should require the H2H hit rate alone (not the blended overall rate) to meet the 75% 3PM floor threshold before emitting. Wembanyama's 3PM T1 had an H2H of 8/9 (89%) and an overall blended rate around 75-78% after VOLATILE penalty — both cleared the floor. But his WCF series variance (0-for-5 in G5 after 3-3-3 in G2-G4) is a within-series cold signal that the current VOLATILE tag does not fully capture. Consider adding a within-series 3PM cold check: if the player has 0 3PM in the most recent series game, apply an additional -3pp on top of the existing VOLATILE penalty for the next game's 3PM pick.
3. The 3PM prop type is 1/3 (33.3%) on this slate, the weakest category, driven by two injury-event misses (Fox) and one variance miss (Wembanyama). This is not a systematic selection error requiring rule changes, but it reinforces the existing directive to treat 3PM non-iron_floor picks as structurally lower-confidence. Confirm that the 80% non-iron_floor cap on 3PM props is functioning as designed and is not being inflated by H2H rates that blend regular-season and early-series data — for WCF/ECF games, the within-series 3PM sample should be weighted more heavily than cross-series H2H.
4. Fox's two misses (PTS T10 and 3PM T1) both carry post_game_event = 'injury_exit' and share the same root cause. When a player has multiple props picked on the same day and a known chronic injury, all props for that player should be reviewed together during the afternoon lineup update — if one prop is flagged for downside (e.g. PTS revised down per FG_MARGIN_THIN), the 3PM prop should receive the same injury-awareness review even if the injury tag is NOT_LISTED. The current lineup_update logic revised confidence_pct for Fox's 3PM from 75% to 75% (unchanged) with reasoning that Williams has no defensive impact — this was correct reasoning about the lineup change but missed the more important Fox-specific ankle risk entirely.
