## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Institute a mandatory tier step-down for PTS picks where min_floor < 24 AND the pick tier is T15 or higher: LaMelo Ball (min_floor=22.9, T15 missed by 1) and Flagg (FG_COLD -18%, T15 missed by 1) both fell just below threshold — dropping to T10 in these cases costs little upside and eliminates the near-miss exposure. Apply this as a rule: min_floor < 24 on a T15 PTS pick → step to T10.
2. Treat FG_COLD >= -15% as a hard tier-reduction signal, not informational: Flagg's FG_COLD was -18% and his PTS pick was T15 at 70% confidence. The informational-only designation allowed an analytically weak pick to proceed. Recommendation: FG_COLD <= -15% should force a one-tier step-down on PTS picks, matching the existing FG_MARGIN_THIN rule logic.
3. For wing scorers (SG/SF) with a down trend on AST, do not apply iron-floor classification to suppress the VOLATILE penalty: Barrett's AST miss (1 actual, down trend, 32 minutes, 25 points scored) shows that high scoring output does not guarantee assist accumulation. The down trend on AST for wings should trigger a VOLATILE deduction even when the iron-floor tag is present — iron-floor should protect the tier pick, not the confidence level.
4. Add a minutes-exit risk modifier for players whose L10 log contains any game with fewer than 20 minutes played in a non-DNP context: Brown played only 15 minutes despite no pre-game flag. If any L10 game shows <20 active minutes (not a DNP), apply a -5% confidence penalty and note as MINUTES_RISK. This is a distinct signal from min_floor and would have reduced Brown's PTS confidence below the 80% assigned.
5. For 3PM picks classified as VOLATILE with a tough DvP, maintain the existing approach of not applying additional tough-DvP penalties per the 3PM noise rule — the Edwards 3PM miss (1 actual, 7/10 rate, tough DvP) is a clean variance outcome on a 70% confidence pick. The rule is correct; the Edwards miss is within expected 30% failure rate and does not warrant rule changes.
