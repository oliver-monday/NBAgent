## Lessons

- AST props remain the weakest prop type at 60.0% hit rate (6/10), dragged down by three misses: Cunningham AST (0 actual — minutes restriction), Mitchell AST (3 actual, iron-floor 10/10 missed), and Bane AST (1 actual, iron-floor 9/10 missed). The iron_floor tag is providing false confidence on AST props when external suppression factors (minutes restrictions, B2B fatigue, scheme-specific suppression) are present. Iron-floor AST tags should not override situation-specific caution signals.
- Cade Cunningham's PTS and AST both missed badly (6 PTS, 0 AST actual) because he played only 5 minutes — a clear minutes restriction. The post-game news confirms this. Despite a blowout spread context (DET favored by 18.5), the pick was not voided and the tier_walk's blowout-resilient classification was applied. The key failure: the model had no mechanism to anticipate a mid-game minutes cap on a star player in a blowout. This is a model_gap_rule — the large-spread blowout should have triggered a harder skip on secondary-to-primary scorer usage (Cunningham's AST especially), or at minimum flagged that blowout games create highest DNP/restriction risk for primary ball-handlers.
- Nikola Jokic PTS missed severely (8 actual vs 15 pick) despite iron-floor T20 and elite scorer blowout exemption. He played only 25 minutes — in a 28-point DEN blowout, Jokic was almost certainly rested early. The elite scorer exemption incorrectly protected this pick from blowout penalties. The exemption assumes elite scorers keep scoring in blowouts, but when spread_abs exceeds ~15, even elite scorers face minutes compression. A hard minutes-compression rule for spread_abs ≥ 15 should be added — either mandatory tier step-down or hard cap at 74% — even for exempt players.
- Donovan Mitchell's AST (3 actual vs 4 pick) and 3PM (1 actual vs 2 pick) both missed in a competitive CLE win (+7 margin). The iron-floor AST tag (10/10 recent hit rate) gave false confidence — Mitchell had 3 AST in a game where CLE won by 7 and he scored 19. The 3PM miss (1 vs 2 needed) in a non-blowout context with iron_floor should be re-examined: iron_floor 3PM picks at T2 still miss 1-in-8 times and the specific CLE switching-scheme watch item (CLE 3PM DvP rates as soft in aggregate but switching scheme neutralizes perimeter looks) may be suppressing Mitchell's own perimeter efficiency when he plays away from home.
- Desmond Bane's AST miss (1 actual vs 2 pick) on a B2B road game at OKC — the toughest defensive environment available — represents a compounding risk that was insufficiently penalized. The tier_walk correctly identified b2b=90% AST as the key anchor, but OKC's defensive scheme is tough and Bane played 36 minutes (full game) on a B2B road night and generated only 1 AST. The pick was reasonable at 80% but the iron_floor tag on a B2B road game vs a championship-caliber defense should carry an additional -5% or require opp=soft gate, consistent with the AST T4+ gate logic that was already partially in the tier_walk.

## Recommendations for Analyst

1. Add a hard PTS confidence cap of 74% for any player with spread_abs ≥ 15, even for elite scorers with the blowout exemption. Jokic (25 min, 8 PTS in a 28-point DEN blowout) confirms that at extreme spreads, resting happens regardless of star status. The exemption should only waive the spread cap in the 8–14 range, not at 15+.
2. Implement a 'minutes restriction risk' flag for large-spread blowout games (spread_abs ≥ 15): when the favored team's primary ball-handler or second star has a VOLATILE or trend=down PTS tag, downgrade both their PTS and AST by one full tier and cap confidence at 70%. Cunningham (5 min, 6 PTS, 0 AST) is the prototype case — the blowout-resilient tag was inapplicable when the player was actively managed out of the game.
3. Revise the iron_floor AST tag to NOT override the B2B + tough-defense gate for any player not classified as a primary ball-handler (raw_avgs AST < 6.0). Bane (SF, ~5 AST avg) on a B2B road game vs OKC should have had his AST pick gated at opp=soft required. The iron_floor tag is designed for structural minimums, not situational floors — on B2B road games vs top defenses, apply the T4+ gate regardless of iron_floor status.
4. Apply an explicit away-B2B penalty of -5% to all AST picks for non-primary-ball-handler wings (position SF/SG, raw_avgs AST < 6.0). The current B2B rate uses season-wide b2b data that may not disaggregate home vs road B2B games. Bane's miss and the general AST underperformance (60%) suggest B2B road AST picks for wings are structurally weaker than the aggregate b2b rate implies.
5. For the CLE switching-scheme watch item: extend the scope from opponent-facing (perimeter guards scoring vs CLE) to CLE players' own 3PM props when CLE is the visiting team. Mitchell's 3PM miss (1 actual vs 2 needed) in a competitive road game suggests that when CLE plays on the road in competitive games, Mitchell's 3PM generation may be suppressed by defensive attention. Add a road-game -5% penalty to Mitchell 3PM picks when the game is projected CLOSE or COMPETITIVE, bringing the effective cap to 70-72% for T2 picks in that context.
