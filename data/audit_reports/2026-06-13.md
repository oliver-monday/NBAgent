This pattern — multiple shooters going cold simultaneously in a high-stakes closeout game — is a known variance cluster that props cannot individually anticipate, but the concentration of 3PM picks on a single closeout game slate amplified exposure.
- Castle PTS T10 miss (actual 6) was driven by a confirmed ankle injury carried from Game 2 that produced a 1-for-10 shooting performance described as 'going missing.' The quant-side pick was defensible (stepped down from T15 due to FG_MARGIN_NEG, series floor of 13+ in all four prior Finals games), but the ankle injury's suppressive effect on FG efficiency was not captured in injury_status_at_check (NOT_LISTED). The post-game news confirms the ankle was a real limitation — this is a case where the injury context was known in the series diary (Castle ankle injury noted in G2) but not reflected in the pick object's formal injury flag.
- Fox PTS T10 miss (actual 7) was explicitly confirmed by post-game news as ankle-injury-driven — sources note he had been nursing the ankle throughout the postseason. The quant-side tier walk correctly flagged VOLATILE and applied the playoff PTS suppression signal, stepping down from T15 to T10. The miss at 7 points (3 short of T10) in 37 full minutes reflects the ankle's real-time suppressive effect rather than a role or minutes compression issue. This is a case where the structural injury signal existed in the series landscape but was absent from the formal injury_status_at_check field.
- Brunson AST T4 miss (actual 3) in a game where he scored 45 points and won Finals MVP exemplifies the scoring-mode AST suppression pattern at its most extreme. His 45-point closeout performance concentrated his usage entirely on scoring, leaving only 3 assists despite 41 full minutes. The trend=down signal and the auditor's prior note about competitive=67% at T6 being a red flag were correct directional signals; at T4 (80% overall, competitive=91%), the pick appeared structurally sound, but the extreme scoring-concentration game script overrode the AST floor in a manner that the quant's competitive rate did not fully capture for MVP-caliber closeout performances.
- Wembanyama 3PM T2 miss (actual 1) is the most analytically significant 3PM miss: the prior four Finals games showed exactly 2-2-2-2 threes (perfect T2 consistency), and trend=up, yet he went 1-for-6 in the closeout game. The lesson: when overall quant tier selection places T2 below the qualification threshold and the only justification for picking it is in-series streakiness, the aggregate signal deserves more weight — especially for a VOLATILE-tagged shooter in a high-pressure closeout environment.

## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Implement a Finals closeout game 3PM exposure cap: on any game identified as a potential series-clinching game (series score 3-1 or greater), reduce the maximum number of 3PM picks across both teams combined to 2. This slate carried five 3PM picks in a single closeout game and went 2/5 (40%). The concentration risk of multiple 3PM props in a single high-stakes game amplifies correlated variance — perimeter shooting can collapse collectively in closeout environments. Cap total 3PM picks per series-clinching game to reduce correlated exposure.
2. When the quant tier selection places the target tier below the 75% 3PM aggregate floor (i.e., overall quant tier=1 and T2 is being justified by in-series H2H alone), do NOT override the aggregate signal for VOLATILE-tagged players. Apply this rule specifically: if VOLATILE is present on 3PM AND overall quant T2 rate is sub-75% AND the only justification is in-series streakiness, skip the pick rather than using H2H override. Wembanyama 3PM T2 (overall quant tier=1, VOLATILE, H2H override justified the pick) is the direct reference case — 4/4 series streak broke in the closeout game.
3. For players with a confirmed injury noted in the series diary (Castle ankle G2, Fox ankle postseason-long) but NOT_LISTED in the formal injury_status_at_check, apply a mandatory confidence deduction of -5pp to all PTS picks for that player going forward in the series. The formal injury flag and the series diary are two separate data sources; when they diverge (diary confirms injury, flag is NOT_LISTED), the diary signal must create a quant penalty. This would have reduced Castle PTS confidence from 72% to 67% (below the 68% floor, triggering a skip) and Fox PTS confidence from 70% to 65% (below the 68% floor, triggering a skip), preventing both misses.
4. For primary-scorer-playmakers (players averaging 25+ PPG in a series who are also primary AST sources), apply an explicit scoring-concentration game script modifier on AST props: in any game where the player's series-average PTS exceeds 30 AND pre-game spread implies a competitive or close game, reduce AST confidence by -5pp to account for scoring-mode suppression. Brunson's G5 (45 pts, 3 AST) is the clearest illustration — his 80% overall AST T4 rate did not account for the extreme right tail of his scoring distribution consuming his distribution share.
5. Anunoby 3PM T2 was marked top_pick (iron_floor=false, non-eligible) — a data inconsistency noted in the pick object where top_pick=true despite iron_floor being explicitly overridden or absent. Enforce a hard constraint: top_pick can only be set true when iron_floor=true AND the VOLATILE+iron_floor incompatibility rule has NOT fired. Add a validation check at pick submission time that rejects top_pick=true for any pick where iron_floor is false or overridden, preventing this classification error from recurring.
