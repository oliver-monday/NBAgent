## Recommendations for Analyst
1. Add a post-blowout secondary scorer vulnerability note to AST iron_floor picks: even iron_floor AST designations should carry a soft warning when the player is a non-primary playmaker on a team that could produce a 20+ margin game. For NAW specifically, the 82% confidence on T2 AST was justified by quant data, but a 1-assist outcome in a blowout is structurally plausible. Consider applying a mild confidence ceiling (e.g., cap at 78% instead of 82%) for non-primary-playmaker AST iron_floor picks when the opponent is sub-.500 and the team is favored by any margin, to implicitly price in garbage-time risk.
2. Introduce a 'surprise blowout fragility' flag for secondary scorer PTS picks selected at low pre-game spreads (spread_abs < 8) against weak opponents (record-based soft rating): when ATL is favored by only 4.5 vs ORL but ORL is a soft defensive team, the actual blowout probability is meaningfully higher than the spread implies. McCollum's T10 PTS miss (9 actual vs 10 threshold) is the prototype — a 1-unit miss in a 29-point game that a tighter rule could have avoided by stepping T10 down to T8 or flagging a confidence reduction for secondary scorers on heavily favored teams vs confirmed-weak opponents.
3. The CJ McCollum PTS pick had a market_implied_prob of 95.65% against our 70% confidence, representing a -25.65% edge. This extreme negative edge (market is far more confident than our model) should trigger an automatic scrutiny flag — when edge_pct < -20% on a PTS pick, require the tier_walk to show an iron_floor or 9+/10 hit rate before proceeding. McCollum's 7/10 hit rate at T10 does not meet this bar.
4. For Nickeil Alexander-Walker, the system correctly selected PTS (hit: 32), 3PM (hit: 5), and AST (miss: 1). The AST miss in the context of an explosive 32-point, 5-three-pointer game suggests that high-volume scoring games may actually suppress assist production for secondary scorers (shot-creation mode vs. pass-first mode). Consider adding a correlation suppressor: when a secondary scorer's PTS pick is at a high tier (T20+), discount the same player's AST pick confidence by 5% in the same game to reflect shot-creation mode priority.
5. Validate the blowout_secondary_scorer hard skip threshold: the current rule fires at spread_abs ≥ 15, but both ATL misses occurred in a game that opened at spread_abs=4.5 and ended at a 29-point margin. A supplementary rule should be explored: when opp_defense_rating = 'soft' or 'unknown' AND the opposing team is bottom-5 in the playoff standings (e.g., ORL at 40-36 in a bubble position), apply an additional 5% confidence reduction to all secondary scorer PTS and AST picks regardless of spread, to price in the elevated probability of a blowout outcome that the market has not yet priced.
