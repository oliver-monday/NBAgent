- Wembanyama's AST miss (actual 1 vs pick 2, 9/10 hit rate) in a COMPETITIVE game (SA won by 13) with only 16 minutes played is the key signal: 16 minutes is extremely low for Wembanyama even in a moderately comfortable win, suggesting minutes were compressed mid-game for reasons not captured in pre-game signals. - AST props for rim-anchored big men at T2 carry a specific structural risk: assists require sustained offensive possessions late in games. When a player like Wembanyama logs only 16 minutes — nearly half his season average — assist accumulation is mechanically capped regardless of per-minute rate. The system currently penalizes AST blowout risk by -10% confidence but does not apply a minimum-minutes floor check specific to AST props (unlike the PTS min_floor rule). A parallel AST floor check should be considered.
- The SAS floor compression pattern (mean miss margin −5.0 on opponent picks vs SAS) may also manifest internally for SAS starters in games where the lead triggers early rest: Wembanyama at 16 minutes is consistent with a managed-exit scenario in a comfortable win, similar to how blowout logic reduces counting stats. The system's blowout threshold (≥20 margin) did not fire here (margin = 13), but a 13-point win with a 30-minute player logging 16 minutes suggests a managed-rest protocol that sits just below the formal blowout trigger.
